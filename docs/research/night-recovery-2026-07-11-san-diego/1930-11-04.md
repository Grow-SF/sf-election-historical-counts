# 1930-11-04 gubernatorial general — San Diego County: NIGHT COUNT recovered (vacuous 1-precinct floor) + a strong next-day CEILING

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits). This file **replaces** the
earlier "NOT AVAILABLE (SDU embargo-locked)" dossier. That pass tried only Sacramento Union
(embargoed), Santa Barbara News-Press (issue doesn't exist), Long Beach Press-Telegram and
San Bernardino Sun (statewide-only). It never opened **San Diego County's own two dailies**
— the Oceanside Daily Blade-Tribune (`BT`) and the Escondido Daily Times-Advocate (`NCT`) —
nor the **Los Angeles Examiner** (`LAE`, a MORNING paper). All three carry San Diego County
election content for this date, and two of them carry the Governor's race.

- Election: Tuesday 1930-11-04, gubernatorial general (James Rolph Jr.-R, Milton K.
  Young-D, Upton Sinclair-Socialist).
- Target contest: Governor (single-seat). Certified San Diego County contest sum =
  **57,709** (Rolph 41,835 + Young 13,985 + Sinclair 1,887 + Scattering 2;

## Route 1 (headline): Los Angeles Examiner, morning of 1930-11-05 — NIGHT COUNT

- Issue OID `LAE19301105`. Issue URL: https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=LAE19301105
- Masthead gate: PASS — page 4 running head reads "SECTION I—PAGE 4 / LOS ANGELES EXAMINER
  — A PAPER FOR PEOPLE WHO THINK / WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 5, 1930"
  (`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/lae1930_p4_full.png`). Nov 5 1930 was a Wednesday; election day
  was Tuesday Nov 4.
- **Edition-type gate: PASS (morning paper).** In this issue's own words (financial page,
  OCR `mirror/cdnc/LAE19301105/search_San_Diego.txt`): *"…no reports from these centers of
  financial activity in The Examiner **this morning**. The usual comprehensive reports
  covering Wednesday's markets will be found in these columns **tomorrow morning**."* Same
  self-declared morning paper as 1926 (see `1926-11-02.md`).
- **Timing gate: PASS.** Page 1: *"indications of a landslide for the Republican ticket in
  California were more than made good as **late returns early this morning** continued to
  add to the … lead of Mayor James Rolph Jr. over Milton K. Young, Democrat…"* The story
  sitting immediately beside the county table on page 4 ("VOTE MACHINES FIRST TO GIVE BALLOT
  COUNT") fixes press-time to the small hours: *"it was four and one-half hours after the
  polls closed, **nearly midnight**, that the first complete returns from any 'rubber
  stamp' precinct reached Kerr's office. It will probably be late today before the last
  precinct is in."* All county datelines in the issue are **Nov. 4** (election night).

**Contest sum = 144 + 53 = 197 ballots**, 1 of San Diego County's 384 precincts.

### Precincts-reported basis

**1 precinct** of 384, both printed in the table's own San Diego row. Ratio 0.0026 ≤ 1: PASS.
(Note the county's own papers give the 1930 precinct total as **363**, not 384 — see
"Precinct-total discrepancy" below. Either denominator leaves the ratio at ~0.003.)

### ⚠️ Warning: this night count is real but nearly vacuous — do NOT chart it naively

197 ballots / 57,709 certified = **0.34%**. Every gate passes and the figure genuinely is a
morning-paper, night-clocked San Diego County Governor sum. But like 1926's, it measures the
**LA Examiner's overnight wire coverage of San Diego** (one precinct!), not San Diego
County's count — the identical `ap-remote-basis` failure mode, in its most extreme form.
Recommendation to the coordinator: carry it as `ap-remote-basis` and either exclude it from
the chart or mark it `comparable: false`. It also **omits Sinclair** (1,887 certified
countywide), so it is a floor even within its own 1-precinct basis. San Diego County's own
morning paper (San Diego Union) is not digitized past 1922, so no better night source exists
in CDNC for 1930.

## Verdict

**NIGHT COUNT (floor) = 197 ballots** — Governor: Rolph 144 + Young 53, at **1 of San Diego
County's 384 precincts**, per the Los Angeles Examiner's morning-of-1930-11-05 "Governor,
Lt. Gov. Vote by Counties" table, in an issue whose returns are clocked "early this morning"
and whose press-time story fixes the first complete precinct at "nearly midnight". Share of
certified: 197 / 57,709 = **0.34%**. Flagged `ap-remote-basis` — see the warning; this bounds
the *wire's* San Diego coverage, not the county's count.

**Secondary: CEILING = 42,126 ballots** (Rolph 31,686`[?]` + Young 10,440; Sinclair not
printed) at all but 20 of San Diego County's precincts (~94.5%), per the Oceanside Daily
Blade-Tribune's own county compilation, explicitly clocked **"at 2:30 o'clock this
afternoon"** on Wednesday 1930-11-05. NULL share, `comparable: false`. This is San Diego
County's own local press (no `ap-remote-basis` flag), and it is the strongest San Diego
number recoverable for 1930 — but it is unambiguously a next-day figure, exactly the trap
the 1932 correction was written to prevent.

This supersedes the prior "NOT AVAILABLE" verdict: the Sacramento Union embargo is not the
end of the road for 1930. San Diego County's own daily printed a county Governor total, and
a Los Angeles morning daily printed a (tiny) night-clocked one.
