## Numerator: the printed "City Totals" row (section 2.26)

Vision-read from `_crop_citytotals_narrow2.png` (context `_crop_citytotals2.png`):

> 1,596[?] [Debs] 133[?] [Wooley] 84[?] [Needham] 2,179 [Crighton] 1,211 [Richardson] 106[?]"

| Column | Value | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Registered | 4,280 | LOW — [?] |
| Voters (total ballots cast, city) | **3,672** | HIGH — matches prose (see below) |
| McKinley | 2,045 | LOW — [?] |
| Bryan | 1,596 | LOW — [?] |
| Debs | 133 | LOW — [?] |
| Wooley | 84 | LOW — [?] |

**Internal consistency check FAILS**: McKinley + Bryan + Debs + Wooley = 2,045 + 1,596 +
133 + 84 = **3,858**, which EXCEEDS the "Voters" (total ballots cast) figure of 3,672 by
186 (5.1%) — a contest sum cannot exceed total ballots cast. This means at least one of
the four president-column digits is misread (most likely candidates: McKinley 2,045 vs.
a smaller true value, or Bryan 1,596 vs. a smaller true value such as 1,256). Given this
failure, **the four per-candidate City Totals figures are NOT used as the numerator** —
they are recorded here only for a future human high-resolution re-read.

## Cross-check: prose "total vote of the city" (section 2.27)

Verbatim, section `SDDU19001107.2.27`:

> "The total vote of the city was 3,672, which was divided among the candidates as shown
> in the tabulation."

This figure (3,672) is legible at HIGH confidence directly from prose type (not a
crowded table cell) and matches the "Voters" column of the City Totals table row exactly
— strong cross-validation of at least that one number. Because president was the top
office on the 1900 ballot and undervote on it is typically small, 3,672 is used here as
an **approximate upper-bound proxy** for the city's presidential contest sum, not an
exact per-candidate sum.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 3,672 ballots** (San Diego CITY only, using the prose-confirmed
"total vote of the city" as an approximate proxy for the city's presidential contest,
since undervote at the top of the 1900 ticket should be minor). This is a floor on
multiple grounds: (1) it covers the city only, not the county's ~70 outlying precincts,
which the table shows were substantially — if not entirely — compiled by press time; (2)
it is a turnout proxy, not an exact per-candidate sum. The table's own per-candidate City
Totals figures (McKinley/Bryan/Debs/Wooley) were read but FAILED an internal consistency
check (their sum exceeds total city ballots cast by 5%) and are excluded from the
numerator pending a human high-resolution re-read. **No countywide total is printed
anywhere in this issue** — the table's `Grand total` row was left blank by the Union's
typesetters, unlike the 1896 and 1920 issues in this campaign, which each carried an
explicit countywide compilation. Share of certified final (6,931): 3,672 / 6,931 =
**53.0%**, a highly conservative floor bounded by the fact it excludes all county
(non-city) precincts entirely. Verdict: **FLOOR (city-only) / county-wide COUNT GATE
NOT EVALUATED** — the full countywide table exists and was screenshotted in full (see
Gap above) but requires a sharper re-capture (e.g. downloading the CDNC page-image TIFF
directly, or a fresh, correctly-targeted deep-zoom pass) before it can be hand-summed
with confidence.
