## Precincts-reported basis

Five of five San Diego County precincts existing at the time are named and
their figures given (this was the entire county in 1871 — no "X of Y
precincts" framing is used because there were only ever these five). Basis:
**5/5 precincts printed, but 1 of 5 (New Town/San Diego, the largest
precinct) explicitly PARTIAL** — 289 of 515 total votes cast in that
precinct had been counted at press time (56%); the other four precincts are
headed "full returns" in the article's own subtitle. 545 is therefore a
genuine night-count floor, not a complete county count (New Town's remaining
~226 ballots, roughly 44% of that precinct, were still outstanding).

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 545 ballots** in the Governor contest (Booth 310 +
Haight 235), across all 5 San Diego County precincts (San Diego/New Town,
North San Diego/Old Town, National City, Central/Cajon Ranchos, San Luis
Rey), printed in the San Diego Union's Thursday 1871-09-07 morning edition
under "THE ELECTION." The article's own printed "Recapitulation" line
(Booth 310, Haight 235) independently confirms the precinct-by-precinct sum
with zero discrepancy — the strongest possible internal cross-check. The
paper explicitly flags San Diego (New Town), the largest precinct, as only
56% counted (289 of 515 ballots cast) at press time, so 545 is a genuine
floor, not a complete county count. Share of certified final: **42.4%**
(545 / 1,285 certified; certified figure from `denominators.md` 1871-09-06
entry, Tribune Almanac for 1872 p.76).
