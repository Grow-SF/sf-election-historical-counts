## Numerator: "FROM THE COUNTY" precinct compilation (target contest, PRESIDENT)

Every digit below was read directly from page-image crops (never from OCR
alone); every crop was legible with no ambiguous digits.

Lead paragraph (`_ward1top2.png`), vision-read verbatim:

> "The counting of the votes cast in San Diego was completed at 2:45 o'clock
> this morning. In the First Ward 45 votes were cast; Second Ward, 56; Third
> Ward, 175; Fourth Ward, 244; Fifth Ward, 104; total, 624. The returns from
> the First Ward received at this office do not give the vote for District
> Attorney, Justices of the Peace and Constables. The other wards are
> complete and official figures, as are also the returns from the outlying
> points. There was a great deal of scratching and other returns will be
> needed to determine the result in a number of cases."

(45+56+175+244+104 = 624, arithmetic checks; this is San Diego CITY only —
the five wards — and is a distinct, smaller total than the President-contest
sum below, since it counts ballots cast across ALL offices per ward, not the
President line specifically. The closing sentence signals more returns were
still outstanding when this issue went to press — a genuine night-count
floor, not a final count.)

No Butler (Greenback) votes are printed for any precinct in this section —
the paper simply carried no Butler column here (consistent with Butler
drawing only 5 votes county-wide per the certified canvass; likely too small
to register in any of these particular precincts, or omitted by the
compositor). Recorded faithfully as printed: no Butler line exists to sum.

Two wards (3 and 4) show their printed "Total vote" line running 2-3 LOWER
than the candidate-sum for President (175 vs 177 printed-sum diff of 2 for
Ward 3; 244 vs 247, diff of 3, for Ward 4). This is an artifact of the
original hand tally (the "Total vote" line evidently reflects a distinct
ballots-cast count, not a per-office candidate sum) and is recorded exactly
as printed — the numerator here uses the SUM OF CANDIDATES per the skill's
single-seat-contest convention, not the "Total vote" line.

**Contest sum = 549 (Blaine) + 250 (Cleveland) + 13 (St. John) = 812 ballots**
counted in the presidential contest across all 9 precincts printed (5 San
Diego city wards + National City, Chollas, Vicente, and Central precincts) by
the Union's press time for its Wednesday morning edition.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 812 ballots** in the presidential contest (Blaine 549 +
Cleveland 250 + St. John 13), across 9 named precincts (San Diego city Wards
1-5, National City, Chollas, Vicente, and Central precincts), printed in the
San Diego Union's Wednesday 1884-11-05 morning edition ("the counting of the
votes cast in San Diego was completed at 2:45 o'clock this morning"). The
article itself flags additional county returns as still outstanding, so 812
is a genuine floor. Share of certified final (1,965, per `denominators.md`):
**812 / 1,965 ≈ 41.3%**.
