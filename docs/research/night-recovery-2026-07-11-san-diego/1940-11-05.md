**Tooling note:** `cdnc_zoom.js`'s FX/FY point-anchoring was unreliable for this issue —
repeated deep-zoom attempts at FX/FY spanning 0.22 to 0.85 all produced byte-identical
13,128,421-byte captures (the tool silently failed to re-anchor), the same viewer-
anchoring artifact already documented in `1920-11-02.md`'s verification pass for a
two-page-spread issue. Worked around two ways: (1) pairing one working zoom anchor with
`PANX`/`PANY` post-zoom drag-pans (a positive `PANY` moved the view up, revealing content
above the initial anchor — confirmed empirically, opposite of the tool's own inline
comment, which says negative pans up); (2) cropping the 0-click full-page reference
capture directly with `crop_helper.py` at computed fractional coordinates for content the
anchored zoom couldn't reach. Both paths converged on the same digits as the independent
OCR fetches.

## Edition-timing evidence (the critical gate, re-confirmed independently)

Two independent facts must both hold for this issue to count as a night-count source
rather than a next-day ceiling; both were re-confirmed by this agent via a fresh,
independent search query (not the probe's saved text) plus a fresh zoom screenshot:

1. **Routine schedule was evening** (so a routine day-after issue would NOT qualify on its
   own): page-2 masthead boilerplate, `NCT19401106.1.2` —
   > "Published every evening except Sunday and legal holidays, by PERCY EVANS, at 07 East
   > Grand Avenue, Escondido, California."
   Visually confirmed at `nct1940_verify_masthead2.png`.

2. **This specific issue was an explicit rushed special morning edition**: the paper's own
   editorial column, same issue, page 2 —
   > "THE TIMES-ADVOCATE scooped the universe with its newspaper report of the election
   > results when it issued an election special Wednesday morning. The first paper with
   > the results to be delivered over the city of Escondido was the Daily Times-Advocate.
   > And the joke of it is that the edition was a complete scoop on the boss, executed by
   > the Democratic staff of the paper, who had sent home the chief, as they call him, at
   > midnight so he would be fresh and in good shape to handle everything the next
   > morning. The conclusive results of the election on election night disarmed the editor
   > and he thought nothing of the need of a special. At six o'clock Wednesday morning the
   > editor's home phone rang, and he was respectfully asked if he didn't want to come
   > down and look over the special, since it was all ready to go to press."
   Fetched independently via `search:scooped`
   (`mirror/cdnc/NCT19401106/search_scooped.txt`) and visually confirmed at
   `nct1940_verify_editorial2.png` (opening lines legible: "THE TIMES-ADVOCATE 'scooped'
   ... Wednesday morning...").

This directly parallels the 1920 San Diego Union "EXTRA — FIVE O'CLOCK EDITION" precedent:
a routine-schedule paper whose SPECIFIC election issue is independently, first-person
documented as rushed to press in the small hours. By the staff's own account the special
edition "was all ready to go to press" by six o'clock Wednesday morning — squarely within
the pre-dawn/pre-breakfast night-count window used throughout this campaign, not a later
next-day ceiling. Unlike 1932's Blade-Tribune (which was corrected to a null-share
CEILING for lacking any such evidence), this issue supplies the rush-printing evidence in
the paper's own words, naming the specific hour.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 114,410 ballots** in the presidential contest (Roosevelt 64,612 +
Willkie 48,478 + Babson 402 + Browder 331 + Thomas 587), 550 of San Diego County's 596
precincts (92.3%), compiled by United Press and printed in the Escondido Daily Times-
Advocate's rushed special morning edition, put to press by approximately 6 a.m. Wednesday
per the paper's own first-person account. This is a genuine election-night count despite
the paper's routine evening schedule, because this specific issue is independently
documented as prepared overnight and ready for press before dawn.

**Share of certified contest total: 114,410 / 128,110 = 89.3%** (denominator: SOV 1940,
San Diego County President contest sum — Roosevelt 71,188 + Willkie 55,434 + Thomas 684 +
Browder 348 + Babson 456 = 128,110; see `denominators.md`, cross-checked exactly against
Wikipedia's independently-sourced county table).

This is the highest election-night share recovered anywhere in the San Diego County wave
to date (compare 1920's 26.3%), consistent with the probe's assessment that this would
"likely [be] the strongest night-count floor available anywhere in this whole project."
