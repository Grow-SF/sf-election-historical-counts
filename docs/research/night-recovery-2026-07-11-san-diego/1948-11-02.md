# 1948-11-02 presidential general — San Diego County night count: NOT AVAILABLE

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits). A documented dead
end: **no San Diego County night count exists in any digitized source for 1948**,
and — unlike 1932/1934/1944 — there is not even a day-after county figure to
record as a ceiling, because neither county daily printed a county-wide
presidential total at all.

- Election: Tuesday 1948-11-02, presidential general. California ballot:
  Truman-Barkley (D), Dewey-Warren (R), Wallace (Independent Progressive), plus
  minor tickets. **Thurmond (States' Rights) was not on the California ballot**
  and appears nowhere in the returns — the campaign brief's candidate list is a
  national one, not California's.
- Papers with digitized November 1948 content covering San Diego County:
  `NCT` (Escondido Daily Times-Advocate) and `BT` (Oceanside Daily
  Blade-Tribune), both dailies; `CVSN` (Chula Vista Star-News), weekly.
  `SDDU` (San Diego Union) is dark after 1922 and NewsBank has no pre-1970 San
  Diego source (`README.md`, `newsbank-probe.md`), so the county's own
  metropolitan press is unavailable for this era by construction.

## Gate 1 — is there a SPECIAL / EXTRA edition? NO (structurally disproven)

The single highest-yield move in this campaign is to find an overnight extra
(the 1936 `NCT19361104-02` "EXTRA EXTRA"; the 1940 ~6 a.m. special). A positive
structural test rules one out for 1948.

CDNC's month-calendar page (`?a=cl&cl=CL2.<YYYY>.<MM>&sp=<CODE>`) renders each
day's link one of two ways, and the difference is diagnostic:

- a date with **exactly one** edition → a direct issue link, `a=d&d=<CODE><date>`
- a date with **more than one** edition → a day-level *list* link,
  `a=cl&cl=CL2.<YYYY>.<MM>.<DD>`

**Control (proves the test detects a known extra):** NCT's November **1936**
calendar renders every day as a direct `a=d` link *except* `cl=CL2.1936.11.04` —
exactly the date of the confirmed `NCT19361104-02` EXTRA. The signature works.

**Applied to November 1948:**

| Paper | Nov 1948 issues | Multi-edition days |
|---|---|---|
| `NCT` | Nov 1,2,3,4,5,6,8,9,10,11,12,13,15,...,30 (25 issues) | **NONE** — every day a direct `a=d` link, including Nov 2 (election day) and Nov 3 |
| `BT` | Nov 1,2,3,4,5,8,9,10,11,12,15,...,30 (21 issues) | **NONE** |

Retrieval: `https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=cl&cl=CL2.1948.11&sp=NCT&e=-------en--20--1--txt-txIN--------`
(and `&sp=BT`), saved to `/Users/sbuss/.claude/jobs/8f24b289/tmp/sd/nct_nov1948_cal.html`,
`bt_nov1948_cal.html`; 1936 control at `nct_nov1936_cal.html`.

**Neither county daily printed a second edition on any day of November 1948.**
The election-day issues (`NCT19481102`, `BT19481102`) are single routine editions
that went to press before polls closed and therefore cannot carry returns.

## Gate 2 — edition type of the day-after issues: BOTH EVENING/AFTERNOON

Both surviving county dailies fail the morning-paper test on their own mastheads,
so even if they *had* printed a county total it could only ever have been a
ceiling (README, "The morning/afternoon trap"):

- **`NCT19481103`** (Escondido Daily Times-Advocate, Wed. Nov 3): masthead
  "**Published afternoons except Sundays**" (`NCT19481103.1.2`; established in
  `sd-local-papers-probe.md`, Part 2, and unchanged here).
- **`BT19481103`** (Oceanside Daily Blade-Tribune, Wed. Nov 3, Volume XXI):
  front-page delivery notice, its own words —
  > "If paper is not delivered to you in Oceanside by **5:45 p.m.** dial 2181 and
  > a special paper will be dispatched to your home."

  An evening-delivery daily ("Published Every Evening Except Sundays and
  Holidays," per this paper's own masthead as quoted in `1932-11-08.md`).

**Timing evidence in the returns themselves** — the day-after issue's own lead
UP dispatch is **noon-clocked on Wednesday**, confirming the press run was well
into the afternoon of the day after. Vision-read digit-legible at
`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/bt1948_statewide_crop.png` (a crop of
`bt1948_statewide_zoom.png`, itself a 3-click zoom of section `BT19481103.1.1`):

> "TRUMAN CALIFORNIA LEAD MOUNTS — SAN FRANCISCO, Nov. 3 (U.P.) — President
> Truman is continuing to pull away from Governor Dewey in the race for
> California's 25 electoral votes. **At noon — with more than half the votes
> counted in the state** — Truman had a lead of nearly 42,000 votes. Returns from
> 13,894 out of California's total of 16,802 precincts gave Truman 1,093,690
> votes; Dewey 1,051,997 and Wallace 101,400."

Same page also carries "DEWEY CONGRATULATES TRUMAN ... Governor Dewey sent a
telegram shortly after 10 a.m." — i.e. this issue was still taking Wednesday
mid-morning copy. Nothing in either paper is night-clocked.

## Gate 3 — is there a county-wide PRESIDENTIAL figure at all? NO

This is what kills 1948 outright. Both day-after dailies print the *statewide*
presidential tally and *district*/local candidate tallies, but **no San Diego
County presidential total**.

### `NCT19481103` (Escondido Times-Advocate, day after)
OCR: `mirror/cdnc/NCT19481103/search_Truman.txt`, `search_county.txt`.
- Presidential: **statewide only** — the identical UP dispatch ("Truman Leads In
  California By 42,000 Votes ... Truman 1,093,690, Dewey 1,051,997, Wallace
  101,400" at 13,894/16,802 *state* precincts).
- County-wide figures that ARE printed: **county propositions only** (e.g. the
  juvenile-hall/board-of-supervisors measure "being passed by the voters, 50,438
  to 21,388"). Propositions are not a candidate contest and are excluded by the
  wave's single-seat-contest rule.
- Also printed: the 23rd congressional district, the 80th Assembly district, and
  an Escondido-and-adjoining-precincts local table. No county president.
- (Independently reproduces `sd-local-papers-probe.md`'s Part 2 finding for
  1948, which this pass re-ran from scratch rather than trusting.)

### `BT19481103` (Oceanside Blade-Tribune, day after)
OCR: `mirror/cdnc/BT19481103/search_precincts.txt`, `search_San_Diego_county.txt`.
- Presidential: **statewide only** (the noon-clocked dispatch quoted above), plus
  **Oceanside (11 precincts) and Carlsbad (6 precincts)** local presidential rows.
- The one county-wide *candidate* figure printed is the CONGRESSIONAL race, box
  "MCKINNON AND CRANE AHEAD":
  > "County-wide returns indicate Clinton McKinnon is assured election to the
  > 81st congress ... McKinnon had a lead of 23,000 votes in returns from **654
  > precincts**. His vote total was **81,297** to **57,889** for Charles Fletcher."

  **NOT USABLE**: (a) Congress is not the target contest (President is, in a
  presidential year), and (b) the 23rd district is a *district* basis, not a
  county basis. Recorded only to show what the paper did and did not have.
- **This is the exact contrast with 1944**, where this same Blade-Tribune *did*
  print the UP county presidential compilation (see `1944-11-07.md` addendum).
  In 1948 it simply did not carry one.

### Day+2 issues (`NCT19481104`, `BT19481104`) — checked, still nothing
OCR: `mirror/cdnc/NCT19481104/search_precincts.txt`,
`mirror/cdnc/BT19481104/search_precincts.txt`. NCT19481104 carries a *statewide*

### `CVSN19481105` (Chula Vista Star-News) — excluded by rule
Weekly (Fridays: Nov 5, 12, 19, 26), published **three days after** the election
→ a canvass figure by definition, never a night count (README). Checked anyway:
OCR shows only local Chula Vista precinct results ("Truman, Democrat, a 73 vote
majority") and a "Truman-Barkley Committee In San Diego County" mention. No
county-wide presidential table.

## Papers ruled out — the remote-paper AP route (the 1928 fallback)

The 1928 row was salvaged from a remote morning paper's AP county-by-county wire

wire had moved to statewide/national aggregates and stopped moving county tables.

**Tooling note (recorded so the next agent does not repeat it):** CDNC's
*unscoped* (all-titles) date-limited search silently returns "No results" even
for a phrase that provably exists in an in-range issue — a control query for the
bare phrase `"San Diego county"` over Nov 3-8 1948 across all papers returned
zero, although `BT19481103` demonstrably contains it. The date filter appears to
require the `puq=<CODE>` paper scope to take effect. **Do not trust a null from
an unscoped CDNC date search.** All negatives above were therefore established
with the paper-scoped tool (`cdnc_fetch.js <ISSUE> "search:..."`), which works.

## Verdict

**NOT AVAILABLE.** No election-night San Diego County presidential count exists
for 1948 in any digitized source, and no ceiling can be recorded either, because
neither county daily printed a county-wide presidential figure at any point.
Share: **NULL**. `comparable: false`. The row should be carried as a documented
dark year, not as a gap-by-omission.

Three independent failures compound, any one of which alone would be
disqualifying:

1. **No overnight edition.** Neither county daily printed an extra in November
   1948 (structurally proven, not merely unfound).
2. **Both papers are afternoon papers**, and their day-after returns are
   explicitly noon-clocked — so even a county figure would have been a ceiling.
3. **No county presidential figure was printed at all**, by anyone, in any
   edition — so there is nothing to record even as a ceiling.

**Why 1940 was not repeatable.** The 1940 win depended on the Times-Advocate's
overnight special, which the paper's own 1940 editorial describes as a *surprise*
sprung on the editor by his staff — a one-off stunt, not a standing practice. It
did not recur in 1944 (see `1944-11-07.md` addendum) and did not recur in 1948.
Meanwhile the county's own metropolitan press (San Diego Union) is undigitized
after 1922 in every archive this project has probed. **The structural conclusion
for the whole 1944-1960 dark era is that San Diego County night counts are
recoverable only in the rare years when a small-town evening daily happened to
print an overnight extra** — and 1948 is not one of them.
