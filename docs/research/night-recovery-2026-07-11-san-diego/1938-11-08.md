# 1938-11-08 gubernatorial general — San Diego County night count: NOT AVAILABLE

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1938-11-08, Governor (Culbert L. Olson-D def. incumbent Frank F.
  Merriam-R; minor candidates Raymond Haight and Noble also on the ballot).
- Target contest: **Governor** (the single-seat statewide race for a gubernatorial year),
  summed across all candidates on the printed **San Diego County** figures.
- Primary route: Escondido Daily Times-Advocate (CDNC code `NCT`) — the paper that yielded
  1936 (a pre-dawn `-02` "EXTRA EXTRA") and 1940 (a ~6 a.m. special).

## Verdict up front

**NOT AVAILABLE.** Two independent failures, either of which alone is fatal:

1. **No San Diego COUNTY Governor figure is printed anywhere in any accessible source.**
   The one paper with day-after San Diego County content (`NCT`) prints only (a) STATEWIDE
   Governor totals and (b) an Escondido-area precinct table. Its San Diego County box
   enumerates "returns of important offices" — Sheriff, Assembly 78th, Assembly 79th,
   Congress 20th, State Senate 40th, and a county proposition — and **contains no Governor
   row at all** (visually confirmed, see screenshot below). There is therefore no numerator
   to recover, not even a ceiling one.
2. **The edition is a routine EVENING issue, explicitly clocked to Wednesday afternoon.**
   Even if a county Governor figure had been printed in it, it would have been a CEILING,
   never a night count (the morning/afternoon trap; the 1932 fact pattern exactly).

No `-02` extra exists for this election. No fallback paper carries the figure. Gate against
the certified denominator is moot (no numerator) and would be PENDING regardless.

## Hunt for a SPECIAL/EXTRA edition — negative, and checked three ways

This was the highest-yield move available (it is what recovered 1936 and 1940), so it was
run to exhaustion:

1. **No separately-catalogued `-02` issue OID exists for either relevant date.** A scoped
   full-text search (`search:the`, which enumerates every OCR-indexed section for a
   paper+date) returns:
   - 1938-11-09 (day after): **4 hits, all under `NCT19381109`** — `.1.1`, `.1.2`, `.1.3`,
     `.1.4`. No `-02` sibling.
   - 1938-11-08 (election day, in case an election-night extra were dated that day):
     **4 hits, all under `NCT19381108`.** No `-02` sibling.
   This is the same query family that surfaced 1936's `NCT19361104-02`, so it is a
   like-for-like negative, not an untested assumption.
2. **The month calendar is NOT trustworthy for this and was not relied on.** Control test:
   the CDNC November **1936** calendar (`CL2.1936.11&sp=NCT`) lists only Nov 2, 3, 6, 7, 9 —
   it omits Nov 4 entirely, i.e. it omits BOTH the routine 1936 day-after issue AND the
   known `-02` EXTRA. Calendar absence therefore proves nothing, which is why the `-02`
   probe above was done by direct scoped search instead. (For the record, the November 1938
   calendar does look complete — 25 issues, missing only the four Sundays and Thanksgiving —
   and shows no `-02`.)
3. **No self-congratulatory "extra/special/scooped" copy anywhere in the issue.**
   `search:extra` on 1938-11-09 returns exactly **1 hit**, on page 2 — and it is a Quaker
   Oil Heater/automotive advertisement ("the number of **extra** revolutions your engine
   makes"), not an edition boast. Contrast 1936 ("an extra edition which was distributed to
   subscribers free of charge early Wednesday morning") and 1940 ("THE TIMES-ADVOCATE
   scooped the universe..."). Nothing of the kind exists here.

## Edition type + timing evidence (the decisive gate) — FAILS

**Routine schedule: EVENING.** Page-2 masthead boilerplate, `NCT19381109.1.2` (OCR at
`mirror/cdnc/NCT19381109/section_NCT19381109.1.2.txt`):

> "Member of United Press. **Published every evening except Sunday and legal holidays**, by
> PERCY EVANS, at 237 East Grand Avenue, Escondido, California."

Same boilerplate as 1936/1940/1944. On its own this makes the day-after issue a ceiling
candidate at best.

**This specific issue's own returns are clocked to WEDNESDAY AFTERNOON** — the returns
themselves name the hour, twice, and it is mid-afternoon. Verbatim from page 1
(`NCT19381109.1.1`; vision-read from `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1938_afternoon_crop.png`
and `nct1938_countybox.png`):

> "**At 3 o'clock Wednesday afternoon** Grape precinct had not completed its task of
> counting an enormous vote. At that time, however, report was made by workers that the
> count stood 157 for Hurley and 216 for Sweet."

> "**At 3:30 Wednesday afternoon** Supervisor Sweet announced that his tabulations showed
> 9665 for Hurley and 9615 for himself. These figures, he said, included No. 18 in San Diego
> and votes counted at Grape precinct up to about 3 o'clock."

Corroborating clock language in the same issue: "Gov. Merriam and Philip Bancroft conceded
**today**"; "results were received slowly from the various precincts" **Wednesday**; and
the paper's own account of "the rush of Wednesday."

**This issue therefore went to press after 3:30 p.m. on Wednesday, November 9** — roughly
19-20 hours after polls closed. That is the textbook next-day CEILING described in
`README.md` ("The morning/afternoon trap") and is the exact error the `1932-11-08.md`
CORRECTION section was written to prevent. It is emphatically **not** night-clocked.

## What IS printed (and why none of it is the target contest)

Front page `NCT19381109.1.1`, masthead "DAILY TIMES-ADVOCATE / Escondido, California,
Wednesday, November 9, 1938 / Volume XXVII, Number 68," banner headline "MERRIAM, BANCROFT
CONCEDE VICTORY."

**(a) Governor — STATEWIDE only, not San Diego County:**

> "Returns from 8954 of the state's 12472 precincts gave for senator: Bancroft 554,613;
> Downey 637,370, Clement 4257. **Governor — Merriam 606,237; Olson, 694,398; Haight,
> 32,074, Noble, 7740.**"

A state total, useless as a San Diego County numerator.

**(b) The San Diego COUNTY box — no Governor row.** Verbatim, vision-read from
`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1938_sdcounty_crop.png` (headline "IZAC IS NAMED TO RETURN TO
U.S. CONGRESS / MRS. DALEY, RITCHIE WIN OVER OPPONENTS — COUNTY RETIREMENT PROPOSITION
REPORTED CARRYING," byline "By United Press"):

> "SAN DIEGO, Nov. 9. — Sheriff Ernest Dort was re-elected, Ed. V. Izac was returned to
> congress and other contests were not even close in San Diego county returns. **Following
> are returns of important offices:**
> Sheriff (412 out of 483 precincts): Chadwick, 34,085; Dort, 43,410.
> Assemblyman, 78th district (167 out of 176 precincts): Daley, 17,018; Fisher, 14,086.
> Assemblyman, 79th district (152 out of 154 precincts): Henry, 10,764; Ritchie, 19,477.
> Congressman, 20th district (412 out of 483 precincts): Bacon, 31,748; Izac, 48,299.
> State senator, 40th district: Fletcher, 70,129; Keckler, 13,349.
> County employment retirement proposition: Yes, 33,839; no 29,345."

That enumeration is complete and closed ("Following are returns of important offices:" then
six lines, then a rule). **Governor is simply not among them.** The county precinct basis
the box does use, where stated, is **412 of 483 precincts (85.3%)**.

For the record, two single-seat county-scale contests DO appear (neither is the target
contest, and both are afternoon-clocked ceilings anyway):
- Sheriff (a genuinely county-wide single-seat office): 34,085 + 43,410 = **77,495** at
  412/483 precincts.
- State senator, 40th district — under California's 1926 "federal plan" this district was
  San Diego County itself: 70,129 + 13,349 = **83,478**, but with **no precinct basis
  printed**, so it cannot be gated.
Neither is substituted for Governor: the campaign's rule is one named single-seat contest
(Governor in a gubernatorial year), not "the largest county contest available."

**(c) Escondido-area precinct table** ("How Escondido Voted In Tuesday's Election"): Merriam
849, Olson 738 for the city's seven precincts, plus San Marcos, Citrus, San Pasqual, Bear
Valley, Valley Center, Oakdale. Hyper-local, a few thousand votes — not a county figure.

**(d) Day+2 checked too, same result.** `NCT19381110` (Thursday, day+2) again prints only a
STATEWIDE Governor total ("Returns from 11,06[x] of the state's 1142[x] precincts gave:
Governor Merriam, 974,235; Olson, 1,138,378; Haight, 42,474; Noble, 15,233") plus more
Escondido-area precincts (Grape, Orange). **Still no San Diego County Governor total** — so
the county Governor figure is not merely absent from the night edition, it never appears at
all. (A day+2 figure would be a canvass number regardless, per `README.md`.)
