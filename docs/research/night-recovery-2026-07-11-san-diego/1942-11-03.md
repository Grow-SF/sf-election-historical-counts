# 1942-11-03 gubernatorial general — San Diego County: CEILING (not a night count)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1942-11-03, Governor (Earl Warren-R def. incumbent Culbert L. Olson-D;
  Fred Dyster-Prohibition and Nathan T. Porter-Townsend also on the county ballot).
- Target contest: **Governor**, all candidates, San Diego County.
- Day-after issue: Escondido Daily Times-Advocate (CDNC code `NCT`), Wednesday, November 4,
  1942, issue OID `NCT19421104`, "Established 1912 — Thirty-first Year / Escondido,
  California, Wednesday, November 4, 1942 / Volume XXXI, Number 63." Banner headline:
  "REPUBLICANS REGAIN POWER IN STATE." Issue URL:
  https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=NCT19421104

## Verdict up front

**CEILING = 51,600 ballots** in the Governor contest (Warren 29,801 + Olson 21,799), at
**461 of 734 San Diego County precincts (62.8%)**. **Share: NULL. `comparable: false`.**

This is a genuine county-wide, single-seat Governor total — unlike 1938, the figure exists
and is cleanly legible. But it **fails the night-count gate on both prongs**, and decisively
so on the returns' own clock phrase: the box states the precinct reports "were being
tabulated rapidly **Wednesday afternoon**." This is the 1932 fact pattern exactly (an
evening paper's routine day-after issue, afternoon-clocked), and it is recorded the way
`1932-11-08.md`'s CORRECTION section and RUNBOOK 5.2 require: as a documented ceiling with a
null share, never as a night count.

## Edition type + timing evidence (the decisive gate) — FAILS on both prongs

**Prong (a) — the issue is a routine EVENING edition, not a morning paper and not a
special.** Page-2 masthead boilerplate, `NCT19421104.1.2`:

> "Member of United Press. **Published every evening except Sunday and legal holidays**, by
> PERCY EVANS, at [2]37 East Grand Avenue, Escondido, California."

Unchanged from 1936/1938/1940/1944.

**Prong (b) — the returns' own clock phrase is AFTERNOON.** Verbatim from the "LATEST COUNTY
ELECTION RETURNS" box, page 1 (`NCT19421104.1.1`; vision-read from
`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1942_latest461_crop.png`):

> "Latest reports from San Diego, where precinct reports were being tabulated rapidly
> **Wednesday afternoon**, gave the following figures of general election interest:"

Corroborating clock evidence inside the same issue, all pointing to a Wednesday-afternoon
press time:
- Warren "won the governorship ... by a margin of more than 200,000 votes **today**" (a
  Wednesday-datelined UP lead, "SAN FRANCISCO, Nov. 4").
- Mayor Percy J. Benbough's death is reported: "was taken by death suddenly **this
  morning**" (dateline "SAN DIEGO, Nov. 4") — i.e. the issue was still taking copy after
  Wednesday **morning**, so it cannot have gone to press before dawn.
- Jordan "gained a strong lead over Democrat Paul Peek on the basis of **nearly complete
  returns**" — a state of the count many hours past the election-night plateau.

## Hunt for a SPECIAL/EXTRA edition — negative, checked to exhaustion

This is the move that recovered 1936 and 1940 from this exact paper, so it was pushed hard:

1. **No separately-catalogued `-02` issue OID for either date.** `search:the` (which
   enumerates every OCR-indexed section for a paper+date, and is the query family that
   surfaced 1936's `NCT19361104-02`) returns:
   - 1942-11-04 (day after): **4 hits, all under `NCT19421104`** — `.1.1` … `.1.4`. No `-02`.
   - 1942-11-03 (election day, in case a night extra were dated then): **4 hits, all under
     `NCT19421103`.** No `-02`.
2. **`search:extra` on 1942-11-04 = 0 hits across the whole issue.** Not one occurrence of
   the word. (In 1938 the same query at least returned a false positive from a car ad; here
   the word is simply absent.)
3. **`search:scooped` = 0 hits.** Contrast 1940's "THE TIMES-ADVOCATE scooped the
   universe..." editorial.
4. **`search:"election special"` = 3 hits, all false positives** — inspected individually:
   an OPA gasoline story ("**special** discounts at their local gas station"), a machine-shop
   ad ("**special** purpose machinery"), a physician's ad ("**Special** attention to
   Obstetrics"), and a Kiwanis notice ("a very **special** program"). No edition boast of any
   kind.

Contrast with the two wins from this same paper: 1936 had a `-02` "EXTRA EXTRA" front page
plus the paper's own account of "an extra edition which was distributed to subscribers free
of charge early Wednesday morning"; 1940 had the "scooped the universe" editorial naming
"six o'clock Wednesday morning." **1942 has none of it.** The special-edition behaviour is
year-specific, not structural, exactly as `sd-local-papers-probe.md` warned.

## The county-wide GOVERNOR figures (two boxes, same issue, different bases)

The issue prints the target contest **twice**, at two different precinct counts. Both were
vision-read from digit-legible zoom crops.

### Box A — "LATEST COUNTY ELECTION RETURNS" (the last pre-press count) → the CEILING

Source: `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1942_latest461_crop.png`. Verbatim:

> "Latest reports from San Diego, where precinct reports were being tabulated rapidly
> Wednesday afternoon, gave the following figures of general election interest:
> **(461 precincts out of 734)**
> **Governor — Warren, 29,801; Olson, 21,799.**
> Lieutenant Governor — Houser, 24,554; Patterson, 26,614.
> Secretary of State — Jordan, 24,498; Peek, 23,579.
> Attorney General — Ware, 24,737; Kenny, 22,363.
> Congressman — Izac, 25,883; Abbey, 24,595.
> State senator — Fletcher, 27,984; Dail, 21,501.
> Measure No. 1 — Yes, 18,698; No, 14,450."

| Contest | Candidate | Votes |
|---|---|---|
| Governor | Earl Warren (R) | **29,801** |
| Governor | Culbert L. Olson (D) | **21,799** |
| | **Sum (as printed)** | **51,600** |

**Arithmetic: 29,801 + 21,799 = 51,600**, at **461 of 734 precincts (62.8%)**.

Caveat, stated plainly: this box prints only the **two major candidates**. The minor
candidates (Dyster, Porter) are omitted here, though they exist on the county ballot and
appear in Box B. In Box B they draw 404 of 23,021 votes (1.76%), so a true all-candidate
county sum at 461 precincts would be very roughly ~52,500. That derived number is **not**
used: only the printed 51,600 is recorded, and it is a ceiling anyway.

### Box B — "incomplete unofficial returns from San Diego county," 216 of 734 precincts

Source: `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1942_official216_crop.png`. Verbatim:

> "Following are the incomplete unofficial returns from San Diego county:
> **216 PRECINCTS OF 734**
> **GOVERNOR**
> Earl Warren (R) .......... **12,586**
> Culbert L. Olson (D) ..... **10,031**
> Fred Dyster (P) .......... **156**
> Nathan T. Porter (T) ..... **248**"

| Candidate | Votes |
|---|---|
| Earl Warren (R) | 12,586 |
| Culbert L. Olson (D) | 10,031 |
| Fred Dyster (Prohibition) | 156 |
| Nathan T. Porter (Townsend) | 248 |

**Arithmetic: 12,586 + 10,031 + 156 + 248 = 23,021**, at **216 of 734 precincts (29.4%)**.

This is the **complete-slate** figure but at a much earlier/lower precinct basis. It carries
**no clock phrase of its own**. It is an earlier snapshot than Box A, printed in the same
afternoon issue — plausibly the overnight state of the count, but **that is speculation and
is NOT claimed**: with no clock phrase and a routine evening edition, it fails the gate
exactly as Box A does. It is recorded here for completeness and as a lead (see
human-verification ask #5), not as a night count.

The same box also carries the full down-ballot slate at 216/734 (Lt. Gov. Houser 10,238 /
Patterson 12,202; Sec. State Jordan 10,303 / Peek 10,164 / Pettis Perry (C) 988; Controller
Riley 18,871 / Whitney 1,364; Treasurer Johnson 18,946 / May 1,080; Attorney General Ware
9,391 / Kenny 11,228 / Watson 1,522; Board of Equalization Bonelli 17,276 / Schmidt 2,616;
Congressman Abbey 10,404 / Izac 11,787; State Senator Fletcher 11,819 / Dail 9,817), plus
assembly districts at their own separate bases (78th: 117 of 283; 79th: 73 of 237; 80th:
29 of 214). None of these is the target contest.

### Not used: the Escondido-area precinct table

"Tabulation Of Votes In Escondido And Precincts Of Immediate Area" — the ten Escondido city

figure.

## Precincts-reported basis

- **Headline ceiling (Box A): 461 of 734 San Diego County precincts = 62.8%.** Ratio ≤ 1,
  gate PASS. The basis is printed in the same box as the votes, so numerator and precinct
  basis share one printed source.
- Box B: 216 of 734 = 29.4%. Ratio ≤ 1, gate PASS.
- Note the county's precinct count grew from 483 (1938) to 734 (1942) — a plausible jump for
  wartime-boom San Diego, and consistent with 752 in 1944 (`1944-11-07.md`). This
  cross-check supports that 734 is read correctly.

## Verdict

**CEILING, not a night count.** Ceiling = **51,600** ballots in the Governor contest (Warren
29,801 + Olson 21,799), 461 of 734 San Diego County precincts (62.8%), printed in the
Escondido Daily Times-Advocate's routine EVENING edition of Wednesday 1942-11-04, with the
returns explicitly clocked to **Wednesday afternoon** — roughly 16-20 hours after polls
closed. The true election-night plateau was **lower**, by an unknown amount.

- share: **NULL**
- `comparable: false`
- floor reported: **51,600** (as an upper bound only; not usable as a night share), i.e.
  **≤ 58.4%** of the certified 88,307. The true election-night plateau was lower, by an
  unknown amount.
- confidence: secondary
- denominator gate: **PASS** — certified SD county Governor contest total = **88,307**
  (SOV 1942 p.4, archive.org `stateofcaliforn194050cali` page n193; see `denominators.md`)

Recorded per RUNBOOK 5.2 and the `1932-11-08.md` precedent. This row should be carried with
a NULL share and excluded from the chart, exactly like 1932 and 1944.

1. `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1942_latest461_crop.png` — **the headline ceiling.** Claimed:
   basis **"(461 precincts out of 734)"**, and **Governor — Warren 29,801; Olson 21,799**.
   **Fail criterion:** any digit differs. (Sum: 29,801 + 21,799 = **51,600**; recompute if
   any digit changes.)
2. `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1942_latest461_crop.png` — **the load-bearing timing
   evidence.** Claimed the box reads "precinct reports were being tabulated rapidly
   **Wednesday afternoon**." **Fail criterion:** if it actually reads "morning," or gives an
   a.m. hour, the CEILING verdict must be revisited — that word alone is what demotes this
   figure from a candidate night count. This is the single most important read in the
   dossier.
3. `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1942_official216_crop.png` — the complete-slate box. Claimed
   basis **"216 PRECINCTS OF 734"** and Warren **12,586**, Olson **10,031**, Dyster **156**,
   Porter **248**. **Fail criterion:** any digit differs. (Sum: 12,586 + 10,031 + 156 + 248
   = **23,021**.)
4. `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1942_p1_full.png` — masthead. Claimed "Wednesday, November 4,
   1942, Volume XXXI, Number 63." **Fail criterion:** any different date or day-of-week.
5. **Open lead (would NOT overturn the verdict, but would sharpen it):** Box B's 216/734
   figure carries no clock phrase. If independent evidence ever establishes that the
   216-precinct compilation was the *overnight* state of the count (e.g. the San Diego Union
   of 1942-11-04 printing a matching 216-precinct county table in its morning edition), then
   23,021 would become a defensible night-count floor for 1942. The San Diego Union is not
   digitally reachable (CDNC `SDDU` ends 1922; NewsBank has no pre-1970 San Diego Union —
   `newsbank-probe.md`), so this is a **microfilm-only** lead, the same disposition as 1914,
   1922, and 1938.
6. **Denominator — RESOLVED (2026-07-12).** Certified 1942 San Diego County Governor
   contest total = **88,307** (Olson 35,113 + Warren 51,709 + Porter 906 + Dyster 579),
   from the CA Secretary of State Statement of Vote, General Election November 3, 1942,
   printed p.4, read from the page image at
   https://archive.org/download/stateofcaliforn194050cali/page/n193_w1400.jpg; statewide
   totals sum to the volume's own printed 2,234,545 Governor citation, and Wikipedia's
   SOV-cited county table agrees digit-for-digit. Recorded in `denominators.md` (1942
