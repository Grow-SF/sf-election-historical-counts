## Why 1956 fails (same structural reason as 1952)

In 1950 both county papers ran an identical United Press **county** roll-up ("In 872
precincts of the county's 1,052, Warren had 100,482 to 51,586 for Roosevelt"; "SAN DIEGO
COUNTY VOTING RECORD"). By 1952 and 1956 that wire product is gone: each paper prints its own
city's precincts, its congressional district (now cross-county, so not a county proxy), and
the national picture. The county-wide newspaper table — the format this whole campaign
depends on — had disappeared from San Diego County's own local press by the mid-1950s, not
just from the big remote dailies. 1950 is the last year in this assignment's window where a
county-wide figure was printed at all.
