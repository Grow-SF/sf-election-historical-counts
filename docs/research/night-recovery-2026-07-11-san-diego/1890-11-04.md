## Method note: this issue reports mostly MAJORITIES, not raw votes

Unlike 1886 (full precinct-by-precinct candidate tallies) and 1920 (a
73-precinct compiled sum), the 1890 Union's county-precinct reporting style is
almost entirely **"Markham's majority is N, Reddick's is M, Bowers' is..."** —
a winning margin per office, not the two (or more) candidates' raw vote
counts. A margin cannot be converted to a floor vote count without knowing the
loser's total, so per the SKILL's discipline (count only what is actually
printed for the target contest), majority-only precincts are **excluded** from
the sum. Only precincts that print an explicit **Markham N, Pond M** pair (or
a tie, "Markham and Pond received/tied on N votes each") are counted.

In the day-after article (2.13), the county-precinct sub-section runs: Perris,
Del Mar, Escondido City, Lakeside, National City, Encinitas, Paradise — in
that order, immediately after the ward-by-ward PROSE summary (no ward vote
table is printed anywhere in this issue or its day+2 follow-up; City wards are
narrated only as "First Ward has gone Republican by a safe majority," etc.,
with zero digits, so city wards contribute nothing countable).

## Precincts-reported basis

Only 3 named county precincts print a countable Governor vote pair in the
day-after issue, out of a much longer list of named precincts in the same
article (Escondido City, National City, Encinitas, Paradise — all excluded per
the table above) plus a further ~30 precincts in the day+2 follow-up article
that are majority-only (El Cajon, Bernardo-area, Barona, Julian, Escondido
Precinct, Oceanside, Chula Vista, Murrieta, Tia Juana, San Pasqual, Descanso,
Vista, Fallbrook, San Onofre, San Marcos, Carlsbad, San Luis Rey, "Jamul,"
etc.) plus 3 precincts that DO print raw ties in the day+2 issue (Linda Vista:
Markham 18/Pond 18; Pala: Markham 26/Pond 26; Descanso: Markham 21/Pond 21) —
**these day+2 figures are NOT added to the night-count floor**, since the
metric is defined on the morning-after (day-after) paper only; they are
recorded here purely as corroborating context that the paper's own convention
(majority-first reporting) continues into day+2 and that when it does print
raw pairs, the numbers are in the same 15-45 vote range as the day-after
precincts — i.e. internally consistent small rural precincts, no reason to
suspect the 3 counted rows are anomalous.

San Diego City's 5+ wards contribute **zero** countable Governor votes to this
floor: the day-after issue narrates ward outcomes in prose only ("First Ward
has gone Republican by a safe majority... Fourth Ward is safely Republican...
The Fifth was very close, but gave a Republican margin... The count in the
Sixth [ward] was still going on at 3:45... The Seventh Ward was not
satisfactory... Pond ran ahead of Markham several votes [there]"), and the
day+2 "Vote by Wards" table (`SDDU18901106.2.14`, confirmed by
`sd_18901106_p5_wardtable_top.png`) is explicitly a COUNTY-office table
(starts at "Superior Judges—") with no Governor row at all.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 184 ballots** in the Governor contest (Markham 103 +
Pond 81), from 3 San Diego County precincts (Perris, Del Mar, Lakeside)
that printed an explicit Markham/Pond vote pair in the Union's Wednesday-
morning (day-after) edition. This is a much thinner floor than 1886's or
1920's because the paper's 1890 local-returns convention reports nearly all
OTHER precincts (and all 5+ city wards) as **majorities/margins only**, which
are not convertible to a vote-count floor and are therefore excluded rather
than estimated. Share of certified contest total: 184 / 7,306 = **2.5%**
(denominator from `denominators.md`, CA Blue Book 1891 p.88).
