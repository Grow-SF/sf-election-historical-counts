# 1922-11-07 gubernatorial general — San Diego County night count (Sacramento Union "A. P. State Returns By Counties," 1922-11-08)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1922-11-07, Governor. Candidates on the CA ballot: Friend Wm.
  Richardson (Republican), Thomas Lee Woolwine (Democratic), Alexander Horr (Socialist),
  plus scattering.
- Target contest: Governor (the single-seat statewide contest of 1922).
- Route: the San Diego Union (`SDDU`) has **no November 1922 issues** in CDNC (re-verified
  this session: the Nov-1922 calendar for `sp=SDDU` returns nothing). Recovery went the
  1924/1928 route — an AP county table printed the morning after in another California
  paper — and this time the table exists and is explicitly labelled as such.

## Verdict (short)

**NIGHT COUNT (floor) = 2,703 ballots** in the Governor contest — Richardson 1,929 +
Woolwine 774 — at **36 of San Diego County's 240 precincts (15.0%)**, per the Sacramento
Daily Union's printed **"A. P. State Returns By Counties"** table, Wednesday 1922-11-08.

**Share of certified contest total: 2,703 / 32,894 = 8.2%.**

Flags: `ap-remote-basis` (Sacramento wire table, not San Diego's own compilation — a dimmed
lower bound) and `partial-candidate-sum` (the AP table carries only Richardson and
Woolwine; Horr, the Socialist, is not a column, so the sum omits him).

**Two other San Diego figures were recovered this session and are documented below.
Neither is used as the headline night count, but the first is a material judgment call the
coordinator should rule on:**

## Precincts-reported basis

**36 of 240** San Diego County precincts (0.150 ≤ 1, gate PASS). The reported count is the
table's own "Precincts Reported" column; the 240 denominator is independently confirmed
**three** ways: the SoV's own 1922 precinct column (San Diego = 240, see denominator
section), the San Pedro AP bulletin ("92 city and county precincts out of 240"), and the
Escondido paper ("186 out of 240 precincts").

## Edition-type / timing evidence (the critical gate)

1. **Morning paper.** The Sacramento Union was Sacramento's morning daily (the Bee was the
   evening paper). Its 1922-11-08 flag reads "SACRAMENTO, CALIFORNIA, WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER
   8, 1922" — note it does **not** print the word MORNING that year, unlike the same
   paper's 1914-11-04 flag ("WEDNESDAY MORNING, NOVEMBER 4, 1914", see `1914-11-03.md`) and
   the 1924-11-05 running head used by `1924-11-04.md` ("SACRAMENTO DAILY UNION. WEDNESDAY
   MORNING, NOVEMBER 5, 1924"). The morning schedule is therefore established by the
   paper's own mastheads either side of 1922 plus the issue's internal clock (next point),
   not by the 1922 flag itself. Flagged for human re-read.
2. **Returns clocked to the night.** Page-1 lead (`SDU19221108.2.19`), verbatim:
   > "…according to incomplete returns from approximately one-fourth of all the precincts
   > in the state **tabulated early today**. Only nine counties gave Woolwine a majority and
   > most of these were **in doubt at 3 o'clock this morning**… Richardson issued the
   > following statement **early this morning** from his headquarters in San Francisco…"
   And, on the count's slowness (same issue): "Officials expected to finish the count for
   most of the city precincts by noon today, but it was expected that two or three days
   would elapse before all rural precincts were heard from."
   The AP county table is an even earlier snapshot than these figures (see cross-check
   above), so it is unambiguously a pre-3-a.m. compilation.

**Specials hunted, none found.** The Nov-1922 CDNC calendars for `SDU`, `SPNP` and `NCT`
list exactly one issue OID per publication day — no `-02` extra/special OID on 1922-11-08
for any of them, and no "EXTRA"/"SPECIAL" slug in any masthead read this session.

## The daylight-bulletin question (San Pedro Daily Pilot) — a judgment call for the coordinator

`SPNP19221108`, article `SPNP19221108.2.19` continued on **page 5**. Verbatim, vision-read
from `spnp1922_sd_crop.png`:

> **FROM SAN DIEGO**
> SAN DIEGO, Nov. 8. — Incomplete **figures at daylight** indicated a republican landslide
> in San Diego county. The water power act was badly beaten and the prohibition enforcement
> act led by a few votes. Vote on governor, in **92 city and county precincts out of 240**:
> Richardson **10,908**; Woolwine **3779**.

Governor sum = 10,908 + 3,779 = **14,687 ballots**, 92/240 precincts (38.3%) → **44.6%** of
the certified 32,894.

Why this is NOT recorded as the night count, and why it is nonetheless flagged:

- **Gate (a) fails as written.** The Pilot's own flag reads "**WEDNESDAY EVENING**,
  NOVEMBER 8, 1922" (`spnp1922_p1_full.png`), and its content is same-day afternoon news
  ("arrived here on the big navy transport this morning at 7 a. m. … according to an
  announcement made aboard the flagship California **today**"). It is an evening paper.
- **Gate (b) passes explicitly, and unusually strongly.** The returns are not merely
  undated — the AP bulletin **timestamps its own figures**: "Incomplete figures **at
  daylight**." Unlike the 1932 Blade-Tribune trap (whose figures were clocked "this
  afternoon," i.e. the count itself had grown past the night plateau), this figure carries a
  dawn timestamp regardless of when the host paper went to press. It sits in the same
  pre-breakfast window this campaign already accepted for the 1920 San Diego Union
  "EXTRA — FIVE O'CLOCK EDITION" and the 1940 Times-Advocate special that "was all ready to
  go to press" at 6 a.m.
- The reason no morning paper carries it is simply chronological: a daylight AP bulletin
  lands **after** a morning paper's ~3 a.m. press time, so only an afternoon paper could
  print it that same day.

**Recommendation:** if the campaign rules that an explicitly dawn-clocked wire bulletin
qualifies even when reprinted in an evening edition, the 1922 floor should be raised to
**14,687 ballots / 92 of 240 precincts / 44.6%**, still flagged `ap-remote-basis`
(it is an AP wire figure) and `partial-candidate-sum` (no Horr). This dossier records the
conservative, rule-literal answer (2,703) as the verdict and leaves the upgrade to the
coordinator rather than relaxing the campaign's central discipline unilaterally.

## The ceiling: Escondido Daily Times-Advocate (San Diego County's own daily)

`NCT19221108` — "DAILY TIMES ADVOCATE, ESCONDIDO, CALIFORNIA, VOLUME ELEVEN NUMBER 67,
WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 8, 1922." A **daily** in 1922 (calendar shows Mon-Sat issues), and an
**afternoon** one by its own clock. Verbatim, page 1 (`nct1922_county_crop.png`):

> "Results in this county: In San Diego county, **186 out of 240 precincts have reported**,
> with the following results: **Richardson, 19,526; Woolwine, 6,942.** Sample, 13,963;
> Doran, 10,909. Kempley, 13,978; Connell, 9,908. Kelly, 13,591; Potter, 10,626. Wright
> law, for 13,112; against 10,932. Water and power law, for 8,201; against 15,930. On the
> following questions only 120 precincts of the county have reported…"

Afternoon clock, same issue, same page:
> "the Republicans still control both houses, **as indicated Wednesday afternoon**… The
> Wright prohibition enforcement law was lost, evidently, the vote **Wednesday afternoon,
> as reported up to three o'clock**, being: 86,069 'yes' and 36,499 'no.'… figures coming
> in Wednesday are cutting down the majority and **at three o'clock** it looked like they
> had lost."

This is the 1932 fact pattern exactly (see `1932-11-08.md`'s CORRECTION): an afternoon
paper's day-after issue, whose returns are clocked to Wednesday afternoon, ~16-19 hours
after polls closed.

- **Ceiling = 26,468 ballots** (19,526 + 6,942), 186 of 240 precincts (77.5%), i.e.
  **≤ 80.5%** of the certified 32,894. The true election-night plateau was lower by an
  unknown amount.
- `comparable: false`, NULL share, confidence secondary.

Worth recording from the same issue as corroboration that San Diego County counted through
the night (not as a vote figure): "Election officers **worked all night** counting and
posting the ballots and returns. It was around **seven and eight o'clock Wednesday
morning** when the result was announced in each of the four precincts" (Escondido's own
four precincts).
