# San Diego County LOCAL-paper probe for the 1934-1968 dark era (2026-07-12)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

**Purpose:** test whether San Diego County's own small-town papers (not
Sacramento/LA/Santa-Barbara remote pickups) carry a genuine, NIGHT-CLOCKED
county-wide candidate table for the CDNC dark years (1934-1968, after `SDDU`
goes dark and while `BT`'s afternoon-clocked returns don't qualify as night
counts — see the method warning below).

## Method warning applied throughout (the morning/afternoon trap)

Per the corrected `README.md`: a day-after issue is only a night-count
source if the paper was a **morning** paper (masthead/returns clocked to
the small hours) or if that SPECIFIC issue explicitly says it was rushed to
press early (a "special edition"). An **afternoon/evening** paper's routine
day-after issue is a next-day CEILING, not a night count, even if it prints
exact digits — because the returns have had 16-20+ extra hours to grow past
the night plateau. A **weekly** published days later is always a canvass
figure, never a night count. Every finding below records (a) the paper's
routine edition type (from its own masthead boilerplate) and (b) whether
the SPECIFIC test issue carries evidence of an early/rushed printing.

## Part 2 — Four test elections, `NCT` results

### 1940-11-05 (day-after issue `NCT19401106`, Wednesday) — **HIT, night-clocked, county-wide**

- Masthead confirms `NCT`'s ROUTINE schedule was evening in 1940 too
  ("Published every evening except Sunday and legal holidays," page 2
  boilerplate) — BUT this specific issue is explicitly a **rushed special
  morning edition**. The paper's own editorial column (same issue, page 2,
  "THE TIMES-ADVOCATE scooped the universe...") states: the staff sent the
  editor home at midnight expecting a normal day, then secretly prepared an
  election special overnight; "At six o'clock Wednesday morning the
  editor's home phone rang" to review the special "since it was all ready
  to go to press." This is direct, first-person evidence the issue went to
  press by ~6 a.m. Wednesday — squarely a night-count edition, not a
  next-day ceiling.
- Front page (`NCT19401106.1.1`), UP wire dispatch datelined "SAN DIEGO,
  Nov. 6": **"In the presidential election a total of 550 of the county's
  596 precincts gave Roosevelt 64,612, Willkie 48,478, Babson 402, Browder
  331, and Thomas 587."** Vision-read and confirmed clean/legible at
  `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1940_toppara_crop.png` (crop of
  `nct1940_masthead.png`, zoom `NCT19401106.1.1` FX=0.5 FY=0.05, 2 clicks).
  Contest sum = 64,612 + 48,478 + 402 + 331 + 587 = **114,410 ballots**,
  550/596 precincts (92.3%) — a genuine SAN DIEGO COUNTY (not city, not
  district) presidential total, single-seat contest, all printed columns.
- Same paragraph also carries the 20th congressional district (San
  Diego + Imperial counties combined, 575/679 precincts: Izac 65,459,
  Fletcher 60,954) with a separate Imperial-valley-only breakout (Izac
  6,056, Fletcher 6,761) letting San Diego County's own congressional
  total be derived by subtraction if ever wanted.
- This is the **proof-of-route** capture requested by the assignment.

### 1948-11-02 (day-after issue `NCT19481103`, Wednesday) — no county-wide table found; edition type reverted to routine afternoon

- Masthead (`NCT19481103.1.2`): **"Published afternoons except Sundays"** —
  routine schedule, no special-edition boast anywhere in the issue (checked
  pages 1, 2, 3, 4, 5). A routine `NCT` day-after issue in 1948 is
  therefore a CEILING candidate at best, not a night count.
- Content actually printed: statewide Truman/Dewey tally (13,891/16,802
  state precincts), the 23rd congressional district (San Diego area only,
  510 precincts: McKinnon 60,112, Fletcher 42,998, Steinmetz 1,255), the
  80th Assembly district, and Escondido's own city/nearby-precinct table
  ("HOW ESCONDIDO AND ADJOINING TERRITORY CAST THEIR BALLOTS," a small
  local subset). **No San Diego COUNTY presidential total was found
  anywhere in the issue** (pages 1-5 all checked via `search:"San Diego"`
  and direct page fetch). Verdict: NOT FOUND for this specific race/year,
  independent of the edition-type problem.

### 1952-11-04 (day-after issue `NCT19521105`, Wednesday) — no county-wide table found; routine afternoon paper

- Front page itself states the paper's normal delivery cadence: "IF YOU
  DON'T RECEIVE YOUR PAPER BY 5:45 O'CLOCK PLEASE PHONE..." — an afternoon
  delivery time. Masthead (`NCT19521105.1.2`) confirms: "Subhahel [Published]
  afternoons except Sunday." No special-edition language found.
- Content: statewide Eisenhower/Stevenson AP tally, the 28th congressional
  district (San Diego area + Orange county combined, Utt vs. Van Deerlin,
  184/286 San Diego-area precincts given separately from Orange county's
  293/338 — a derivable San-Diego-only congressional figure, but not
  presidential), and Escondido's own 12-precinct city table (Eisenhower
  2,223, Stevenson 1,011 — hyper-local, not county-wide). **No San Diego
  COUNTY presidential total found.**

### 1960-11-08 (day-after issue `NCT19601109`, Wednesday) — no county-wide table found; routine afternoon paper

- Same "IF YOU DON'T RECEIVE YOUR PAPER BY 5:45 O'CLOCK" afternoon-delivery
  notice on the front page, and the masthead again reads "Published
  afternoon except Sunday" (`NCT19601109.1.3`). A mid-day clock phrase
  appears elsewhere on the page ("As of 12:30 p.m. the statewide count on
  California's controversial propositions...") confirming this issue's own
  content is a midday, not overnight, snapshot.
- Content: statewide propositions tally, national Kennedy/Nixon narrative,
  the 29th congressional district (Utt vs. Woods) in scattered individual
  PRECINCT items (Valley Center 2, Del Dios 2, three Golden precincts —
  each a few hundred votes, not a county aggregate), and a multi-seat
  hospital-district trustee race (excluded by the wave's single-seat rule
  regardless). **No San Diego COUNTY presidential total found**, and even
  if one existed in this issue it would be a ceiling given the confirmed
  afternoon/midday-clocked publication.

## Part 3 — Recommendation

1. **`NCT` (Escondido's Daily Times-Advocate / "North County Times" CDNC
   code) is the highest-value San Diego County local paper for this dark
   era** — dense near-daily coverage across 1940-1960 at minimum, unlike
   every other county paper tested (all either weekly, undigitized, or
   both). Use `NCT`, not `TAD` (nearly empty) or `BT` (evening-clocked +
   has its own 1935-1937 digitization gap).
2. **1940-11-05 is a confirmed, verified WIN**: a genuine night-clocked
   (special ~6 a.m. edition, documented in the paper's own words), San
   Diego COUNTY-wide (not district, not city), single-seat presidential
   contest sum of **114,410 ballots at 550/596 precincts (92.3%)** — likely
   the strongest night-count floor available anywhere in this whole
   project once formally written up as a dossier (compare to 1932's
   corrected/lower Blade-Tribune figure). **This election is ready for a
   full recovery dossier**, not just a probe note — recommend promoting it
   next.
3. **1948, 1952, and 1960 did NOT reproduce the win.** Two independent
   problems compound: (a) by 1948 `NCT`'s ROUTINE schedule had shifted to
   afternoon publication with no evidence of a repeated special early
   edition in any of the three years checked, and (b) even setting the
   edition-type problem aside, none of the three issues prints a San Diego
   COUNTY-wide single-seat contest table at all — only district-level
   (partly San-Diego-County, partly shared with Orange/Imperial county)
   and hyper-local Escondido-area tables. The `NCT` route is therefore
   **year-specific, not structurally reliable** across the dark era; each
   candidate year needs its own check for an explicit early/rushed-edition
   boast AND an actual county-wide table, not just confirmation that the
   paper exists that day.
4. **Untested but promising next candidates**, in priority order: (a)
   re-check `NCT` for 1934 and 1936 specifically — the two elections the
   parallel agent just marked NOT AVAILABLE via `BT`/remote papers — since
   1934 and 1936 are closer in time to 1940's confirmed evening-schedule
   pattern and might show the same special-edition behavior for a
   high-drama national race (1936 was also an FDR landslide, similar in
   kind to 1940); (b) 1944 (FDR's 4th term, another dramatic wartime
   election, plausible candidate for a rushed special edition); (c) a
   fuller day+1/day+2 sweep of `NCT` for 1948/1952/1960 specifically
   looking for a LATER wire dispatch that might carry a county table
   the day-after issue lacked (would likely be a ceiling, not a night
   count, but still worth a quick check before writing off those years
   entirely, and useful as an upper-bound sanity check against the
   afternoon-paper concern in finding 3).
5. **Papers ruled out entirely for this project**: `CVSN` (weekly, always
   post-canvass), `NCSN`, `CJ`, `LJL`/`LJJ`, `SNNC` (no digitized election-era
   content found in CDNC under these codes despite catalog date ranges
   claiming coverage) — do not re-probe these without a reason to think
   the codes above were wrong (e.g., a differently-spelled `puq` code
   found via the live CDNC search-form dropdown rather than the title
   browse page, which this probe did not cross-check).
