## Timing note

The box is explicitly headed "Unofficial returns... this AFTERNOON" —
i.e., an election-DAY afternoon count (polls closed at some point that
day), not a next-morning final compilation. This is earlier/less complete
than the SD Union's own historical morning-EXTRA compilations (1920: 73 of
223 precincts by the 5am extra), but the PRECINCT RATIO here (339/384 =
88%) is far higher than any other source recovered in this wave — likely
because Blade-Tribune press time was later than a big-city morning paper's,
letting more precincts report first.

## CORRECTION (2026-07-12, coordinator review): this is a CEILING, not a night count

The verdict above is **withdrawn as a night count**. Its own stated timing is
impossible: it claims returns "from election-day afternoon of 1932-11-08", but
polls did not close until 8 p.m., so no precinct returns existed on the
afternoon of election day. The article's phrase is "339 precincts out of 384 in
San Diego city and county **this afternoon**", printed in the 1932-11-09 issue,
so the returns are clocked to the afternoon of **Wednesday, November 9**: about
sixteen to twenty hours after polls closed.

The Oceanside Blade-Tribune was an **afternoon (evening) paper**. That is the
methodological trap this row fell into: for a MORNING paper, the day-after issue
goes to press around 2-4 a.m. and captures the election-night plateau; for an
AFTERNOON paper, the day-after issue goes to press around midday and captures a
count that has already resumed its multi-day canvass. The two are not
interchangeable, and only the former satisfies the metric in `README.md`.

**Recorded disposition** (per `docs/research/RUNBOOK.md` section 5.2): a
post-night figure may be kept only as a documented CEILING, never as a night
share. So:

- ceiling = 76,427 ballots (339 of 384 precincts), i.e. **<= 89.8%** of the
  certified 85,150. The true election-night plateau was lower, by an unknown
  amount.
- `comparable: false`. The row is carried in
  `data/research/san-diego-history/sd_night_history.csv` with a NULL share and
  a flag saying why, and it is therefore excluded from the chart (the build
  script skips rows without a share).
- confidence: secondary.

The transcription itself (the five candidate figures, the 339/384 basis) is not
in dispute and remains verified; only its interpretation as an election-night
count was wrong.

**Lesson for the rest of this campaign:** before using any local-paper figure,
establish whether the paper was a morning or an afternoon paper, and read the
returns' own clock phrase ("this morning" vs "this afternoon"). An afternoon
paper's day-after edition is a next-day ceiling.
