Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits). This file **replaces** the
earlier "NOT AVAILABLE" dossier: that pass checked only Sacramento Union (`SDU`), Santa
Barbara News-Press (`SBNP`) and Long Beach Press-Telegram (`LBPT`). This pass opened four

### Precincts-reported basis

**2 precincts**, printed in the panel itself. Denominator for the ratio: San Diego County
had **308** precincts in 1926, per the LA Evening Herald's own county table (the Herald's
Senator table on the same page prints 307 for the same county — a one-precinct
inconsistency inside that paper; either way the ratio is ~0.006). Ratio ≤ 1: PASS.

### ⚠️ Warning: this night count is real but nearly vacuous — do NOT chart it naively

22 ballots / 38,194 certified = **0.058%**. All gates pass, and the figure genuinely is a
morning-paper, night-clocked San Diego County single-seat contest sum. But it measures the
**LA Examiner's overnight wire coverage of San Diego**, not San Diego County's count: the
Examiner simply never got more than two San Diego precincts before press. This is exactly
the `ap-remote-basis` caveat the README attaches to the 1920/1924/1928 remote-paper floors,
in its most extreme form. Recommendation to the coordinator: carry it as
`ap-remote-basis`, and either exclude it from the chart or mark it `comparable: false` —
a 0.06% dot would be read as "San Diego counted almost nothing on election night 1926",
which this source cannot support. The county's own morning paper (San Diego Union) is not
digitized past 1922, so no better night source exists in CDNC for this year.

## Route 2: Los Angeles Evening Herald, 1926-11-03 — CEILING (the substantive figure)

- Issue OID `LAH19261103`, page 16 (`LAH19261103.1.16`). Issue URL:
  https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=LAH19261103
- Masthead gate: PASS — running head reads "A—16 ★ WEDNESDAY — **LOS ANGELES EVENING
  HERALD**: NOVEMBER 3, 1926 — WEDNESDAY" (`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/lah1926_p16_full.png`).
- **Edition-type gate: FAIL for a night count — this is an EVENING paper**, and this is its
  routine Wednesday edition (no special/extra slug anywhere; the CDNC November-1926
  calendar for `LAH` lists exactly one issue OID per publication day, no `-02` extra —
  `/Users/sbuss/.claude/jobs/8f24b289/tmp/sd/cal_LAH_192611.html`). Its own accompanying
  story, "STILL COUNTING VOTE", is clocked to Wednesday: *"Counting of the vote was still in
  progress in most communities of the state **today**. Even with voting machines, San
  Francisco was unable to fully complete its count…"* Per the campaign rule (README, "the
  morning/afternoon trap"; `1932-11-08.md`'s CORRECTION), a routine evening paper's
  day-after figure is a **next-day CEILING**, never a night count.

### The table (vision-read from `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/lah1926_govtable_crop.png`)

Boxed table headed **"Counties' Vote on Governor"**, columns `County— | Prects. In | Total
Prects. | Young | Wardell`:

| County | Prects. In | Total Prects. | Young | Wardell |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| … | | | | |
| San Bernardino | 5 | 229 | 153 | 24 |
| **San Diego** | **109** | **308** | **9,154** | **1,945** |
| San Francisco | 837 | 892 | 67,572 | 44,125 |

**Printed contest sum = 9,154 + 1,945 = 11,099 ballots**, 109 of 308 precincts (35.4%).

**The table prints only two of the three candidates** — there is no Sinclair column — so
even as a ceiling this sum omits Sinclair's San Diego votes (1,759 certified countywide).
The ceiling is therefore a *partial-slate* ceiling: the true Wednesday-midday count was
somewhat higher than 11,099.

Cross-checks in the same issue (context, not summed):
- The parallel boxed table "Counties' Vote on Senator" gives San Diego 109 precincts in /
  **307** total, Shortridge 5,893, Elliott 5,257 — same precincts-in count, one fewer total
  precincts. The 307/308 discrepancy is the paper's, not a transcription error; both are
  recorded.
- Page 16's "LATE SOUTHERN CAL. COUNTIES RETURNS" column, San Diego item: *"Reports from 16
  widely scattered precincts out of a total of 307 in San Diego county indicated that C. N.
  Andrews, Charles B. DeLong and Spencer M. Marsh would win their races for superior court
  judgeships. The count was Andrews, 508; DeLong, 415; Charles C. Haines, 393; Shelly J.
  Higgins, 68; Lewis R. Kirby, 226; Marsh, 452."* — a 16-precinct, multi-seat judicial race
  (excluded), and it confirms 307 as that column's precinct total.

### Ceiling disposition

- ceiling = **11,099 ballots** (Young + Wardell only), 109 of 308 precincts, i.e. **≤ 29.1%**
  of the certified 38,194 as printed (higher once Sinclair is added back). The true
  election-night plateau was lower by an unknown amount.
- share: **NULL**. `comparable: false`. confidence: secondary.

## Verdict

**NIGHT COUNT (floor) = 22 ballots** — Governor: Young 17 + Wardell 4 + Sinclair 1, at **2 of
San Diego County's 308 precincts**, per the Los Angeles Examiner's morning-of-1926-11-03
"HERE'S VOTE OF COUNTIES IN SOUTHLAND" box, whose returns are explicitly clocked "last
night". Share of certified: 22 / 38,194 = **0.058%**. Flagged `ap-remote-basis`; see the
warning above — this bounds the *wire's* San Diego coverage, not the county's count, and
should not be charted as San Diego's election-night share without that caveat.

**Secondary: CEILING = 11,099 ballots** (Young 9,154 + Wardell 1,945, Sinclair not printed)
at 109 of 308 precincts, per the Los Angeles Evening Herald's "Counties' Vote on Governor"
table in its routine Wednesday evening edition. NULL share, `comparable: false`.

This supersedes the prior "NOT AVAILABLE" verdict: San Diego County candidate figures for
1926 DO exist in CDNC, in the two Los Angeles dailies, and they were simply never opened.
