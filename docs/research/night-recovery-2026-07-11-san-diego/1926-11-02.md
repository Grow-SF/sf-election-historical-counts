# 1926-11-02 gubernatorial general — San Diego County night count: NOT AVAILABLE

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits). Route
attempted: Sacramento Daily Union (SDU) morning-after issue, per
`cdnc-county-table-probe.md`; backup papers Santa Barbara News-Press (SBNP)
and Long Beach Press-Telegram (LBPT) also checked.

- Election: Tuesday 1926-11-02, gubernatorial general (C.C. Young-R vs.
  Justus S. Wardell-D, plus Upton Sinclair-Socialist).
- Day-after issue: Sacramento Union, Wednesday morning 1926-11-03, issue OID
  `SDU19261103`, Volume 238, Number 27621. Masthead confirmed: "SACRAMENTO
  UNION. WEDNESDAY MORNING, NOVEMBER 3, 1926." (front page, headline "YOUNG
  AND SHORTRIDGE WIN BY BIG VOTE").

## What was checked

1. **Front-page state tabulation article** (`SDU19261103.1.1`, "Newly Elected
   Executive Pleased at G.O.P. Triumph," continues to `.1.3`): gives ONLY a
   statewide AP total for governor — "returns from 1507 precincts, which
   gave: Young 73,875, Wardell 28,105, Upton Sinclair (socialist) 751" — no
   county breakdown anywhere in this article.
2. **`search:"State Election Table"`** (the format that worked cleanly for
   1924): 1 false-positive hit (OCR mismatch on an unrelated egg-production
   item), confirmed by direct inspection — **no county-grid "State Election
   Table" was printed in this issue** (unlike 1924's SDU19241105.1.2).
3. **`search:"Alameda"`** (a control check — a printed CA county table would
   repeat "Alameda" as its first alphabetical row/header): only 1 hit,
   unrelated to a candidate table (a ballot-measure precinct count for
   Alameda county in a different context). Confirms no county-by-county
   candidate table exists in this issue.
4. **`search:"Election Bulletins"`** (the national AP wire-flash box, 2
   hits, `SDU19261103.1.1` + `.1.2`): a page-1-to-page-2 column of one-line
   state-by-state briefs (Rhode Island, Indiana, South Dakota, Iowa,
   Kentucky, North Dakota, Oregon, New Mexico, Arizona, Idaho, Michigan,
   Pennsylvania, Massachusetts, Ohio, Maryland...) — **no California entry**
   (the home-state paper evidently didn't need a wire flash for its own
   state). The one "San Diego" hit `search:"San Diego"` returned (via
   `SDU19261103.1.2`) traced, after zoomed inspection, to a DIFFERENT box
   entirely (see next item).
5. **`search:"San Diego"`** (1 hit, `SDU19261103.1.2`): zoom-read to
   `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/sd1926_p2_propcol.png` — this is the
   **"VOTE INDICATES WRIGHT REPEAL" ballot-measure box**, which prints
   PROPOSITION yes/no counts (gasoline tax, racing initiative, Wright Act
   repeal, Bible in schools, water and power, reapportionment) for a run of
   outlying counties on PARTIAL precincts: Riverside (24), Santa Clara (8),
   San Bernardino (1), and **San Diego (13 precincts)** — verbatim: "Thirteen
   precincts, San Diego county: No. 4—Gasoline tax, yes 200, no 264 ... No.
   6—Racing, yes 263, no 349 ... No. 9—Wright act, yes 308, no 345, no 189
   [sic, garbled] ... No. 17—Bible in schools ... no 345 ... No. 18—Water and
   power ... no 379 ... No. 20—Reapportionment ... yes 119, no 354 ... No.
   28—Legislative reapportionment ... yes 259, no 195." **This is
   PROPOSITION data only — no candidate (Governor/Senator) vote counts for
   San Diego County appear anywhere in this box or issue.**

## Verdict

**NOT AVAILABLE.** No San Diego County candidate-vote figure for the 1926
governor's race was found in any of the three papers checked (SDU, SBNP,
LBPT). The Sacramento Union printed only (a) a statewide governor total with
no county breakdown, and (b) a San Diego County PROPOSITION-only partial
tally (13 precincts, ballot measures, no candidates) — confirming the paper
covered San Diego County returns in SOME form that night, but never printed
its Young/Wardell/Sinclair vote split. This differs from 1924 and 1928,
where a genuine county-level candidate table or AP flash existed; 1926 sits
in a gap where the Union's format for that specific night happened not to
carry it (or carried it on a page/edition not yet located — see asks below).
No floor sum, no gate evaluation possible (nothing to gate).
