## Transcription — Governor, day-after table (45 of 95 precincts, to 3 a.m.)

From `sd_19101109_p8_gov2.png` (vision read; OCR of 2.67 garbles all digits):

| Candidate | Votes |
|---|---|
| Johnson (R) | 1,958 |
| Bell (D) | 1,169 |
| Wilson (S) | 614 |
| Meads (Proh) | not printed |
| **Contest sum (as printed)** | **3,741** |

Internal consistency check on Johnson's third digit (5 vs 3): the p1 article says
Johnson "surpassed the vote of every other candidate on the state ticket"; State Printer
Shannon (R) reads 1,947 in the same table, so Johnson must exceed 1,947 → 1,958 (not
1,938). Other same-table context lines (all vision-read): Lt. Governor Wallace (R) 1,640
/ Spellacy (D) 800 / Wheeler (S) 580; Controller Nye (R) 2,268 (the article notes
Johnson fell below only Nye among contested lines and unopposed county offices);
Sheriff Jennings (R) 1,858 / Brodnax (D) 955; District Attorney Utley (R) 1,839 /
Wells (D) 914; Amendment No. 1 Yes 479 / No 368.

## Arithmetic

- Night floor = 1,958 + 1,169 + 614 = **3,741 ballots counted in the Governor contest**
  across the 45 precincts complete by 3 a.m. E+1. (Meads' Prohibition votes were not
  printed, so the true contest count is slightly higher; floor stands.)

## Precincts-reported basis

- 45 of 95 precincts (30 city + 65 country per day+2 article) complete by 3 a.m.;
  incomplete partial returns were in hand from "as many more" but not printed as totals.
  Ratio 45/95 = 0.47 <= 1 — gate PASS.
- The count was explicitly slow: "Universal Scratching of Ballots Delays Count in All
  Precincts; Many Boards Will Finish Late Today."

## VERDICT

**FLOOR (night count).** Ballots counted in the Governor contest and printed in the E+1
morning San Diego Union: **3,741** across 45 of 95 precincts (returns to 3 a.m.).
**Share of certified contest total: 3,741 / 9,479 = 39.5% (floor).** The paper documents
that roughly as many precincts again were mid-count at press time and that boards would
"finish late today"; by E+2 morning the printed total had reached 8,736 (92.2%) with the
city complete.
