# 1896-11-03 presidential general — San Diego County night count (San Diego Union, 1896-11-04)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1896-11-03, presidential general. California ballot: McKinley-R,
  Bryan appearing on TWO fusion slates (Democrat/Bryan-Sewall and People's
  Party/Bryan-Watson), Palmer-National Democrat, Levering-Prohibition, Bidwell-National
  Party, Matchett/Biddle-Socialist Labor (per `denominators.md`). The San Diego Union's
  own tables (both the city ward table and the county precinct table) print Bryan as a
  SINGLE combined "Bryan" line, i.e. the paper's own count already fuses the two Bryan
  slates — recorded faithfully as printed.
- Day-after issue: San Diego Union and Daily Bee, Wednesday morning 1896-11-04, issue
  OID `SDDU18961104` (88 sections). Issue URL: https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=SDDU18961104
- Masthead gate: PASS — "THE SAN DIEGO UNION, WEDNESDAY MORNING, NOVEMBER 4, 1896"
  (visible in `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/sd_18961104_p5_zward_up.png`; Nov 4 1896 was
  indeed a Wednesday, one day after the Nov 3 Tuesday election).

## Numerator: the printed RECAPITULATION (target contest, PRESIDENT)

Verbatim, section `SDDU18961104.2.47` (vision-read from `_crop_pres_tight.png`, context
`_zrecap1.png`):

> "RECAPITULATION. Totals of Sixty Precincts Out of 86 Show the Result Without Doubt.
> Following is a recapitulation of the total vote of the city and 42 precincts of the
> county, making 60 precincts in all.
> Presidential—McKinley 3,176, Bryan 3,139; McKinley's plurality 37."

| Contest | Ticket | Votes | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| President | McKinley (R) | **3,176** | high confidence |
| President | Bryan (D + Pop. fusion, combined) | **3,139** | high confidence |

Internal arithmetic check: 3,176 − 3,139 = 37, which matches the printed "McKinley's
plurality 37" exactly — strong confirmation of both digit strings (rules out e.g. a
misread 3,178 or 3,132).

**Contest sum (major tickets only) = 3,176 + 3,139 = 6,315 ballots**, 60 of 86 city +
county precincts (city complete + 42 of 68 county precincts), compiled by the Union for
its Nov. 4 morning edition.

No Palmer, Levering (Prohibition), Bidwell (National Party), or Socialist Labor
county-wide figures are aggregated anywhere in this issue — those tickets appear only as
per-precinct line items (see below), never summed by the paper into a countywide total.
6,315 is therefore a **floor on two grounds**: partial precincts (60/86) and major-ticket-only
aggregation (minor tickets are printed but not tallied by the Union).

## Cross-check 1: general "total vote" line (same article, same 60-precinct basis)

Immediately following the race-by-race recapitulation, the same section prints:

> "Total vote in the city, complete, 3,763; county, 42 precincts, 2,647; total, 6,410."

6,410 sits just above the McKinley+Bryan sum (6,315), a gap of 95 — plausibly the missing
minor-ticket (Palmer/Prohibition/National/Socialist Labor) presidential votes for the same
60 precincts, though the line is not explicitly labeled as presidential-specific (it could
be a general ballots-cast/turnout figure for the 60 precincts covering all races). Treated
as context, not as the headline contest sum, but it is consistent with — and slightly above
— the major-ticket floor, as expected.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 6,315 ballots** in the presidential contest (McKinley 3,176 + Bryan
3,139, the Union's own fused-slate figures), 60 of 86 San Diego city+county precincts,
printed in the San Diego Union's Nov. 4, 1896 morning-after RECAPITULATION. This is a floor
on two independent grounds: (1) only 60 of 86 precincts had reported, and (2) the printed
compilation aggregates only the two major tickets — Palmer, Prohibition, National Party,
and Socialist Labor votes are visible per-precinct in the underlying tables but were never
summed by the Union into a countywide total, and were not hand-summed here either (see
cross-check 3 gap). The general "total vote" line (6,410) suggests the true full-contest
count for these same 60 precincts is only modestly higher (~1.5%). Share of certified final
(7,762): 6,315 / 7,762 = **81.3%** of the FULL countywide certified contest sum, despite
covering only 70% of precincts and excluding minor tickets — consistent with city precincts
(complete) carrying disproportionate weight relative to slow-reporting back-country
precincts.
