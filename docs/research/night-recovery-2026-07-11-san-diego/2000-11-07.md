## Timing check (the decisive issue)

This is the one figure found in the day-after (2000-11-08) or day+2
(2000-11-09) SDUB coverage that gives a raw countable number, and it is
explicitly a **day+2** statement: the article ran two days after the
election, describes a process still expected to run "until Monday" (i.e.,
five days after the Tuesday election), and gives no indication of the count
as it stood at the close of election night specifically. Per the decision
rules: "A day+2 canvass statement is NOT a night floor: record as
ceiling/context with comparable=false semantics, or null."

Because ballot processing is monotonic (the remaining-ballot count can only
shrink or hold steady between election night and day+2, never grow), the
150,000 figure is a floor on ballots STILL remaining at day+2, which
translates to a **ceiling** on the night-count number itself: whatever the
true election-night remaining count was, it was >= 150,000 (more ballots
were outstanding on election night than on day+2, since some got processed
in between), so the night COUNT was <= certified − 150,000.

## Arithmetic (ceiling only, not a floor)

Certified total ballots cast (SoS SOV 2000, `denominators.md`): **978,569**.

978,569 − 150,000 = **828,569** → this is a **ceiling** on the
election-night count (828,569 / 978,569 = 84.7%, but this percentage
describes an upper bound on the night's share, not the night's actual
share).

## Verdict

a day+2 canvass-in-progress statement, not an election-night end-state
statement, and per the RUNBOOK's own convention (section 5.2) a post-night
report may be kept only as a documented **ceiling** with `comparable: false`

(84.7%)**, i.e., the true night count was at most this, but the actual
