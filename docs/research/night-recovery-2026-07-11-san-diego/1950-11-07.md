# 1950-11-07 gubernatorial general — San Diego County: **CEILING, not a night count**

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1950-11-07, California governor (Earl Warren-R, incumbent, seeking an
  unprecedented third term; James Roosevelt-D). Target contest = Governor (the single-seat
  contest for a gubernatorial year).
- Primary paper: **Escondido Daily Times-Advocate**, CDNC code `NCT`, day-after issue
  `NCT19501108` ("North County Times, Volume 39, Number 67, 8 November 1950" in CDNC's
  catalog label; the paper's own masthead reads *Daily Times-Advocate*).
  Issue URL: https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=NCT19501108
- Corroborating paper: **Oceanside Blade-Tribune**, CDNC code `BT`, day-after issue
  `BT19501108`. Same day, same UP county compilation, same digits (see "Independent
  corroboration" below).
- Masthead gate: **PASS** — "FOUNDED IN 1912 — (Weekly in 1886) / ESCONDIDO, CALIFORNIA,
  WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 8, 1950 / VOLUME XXXIX, NUMBER 67". Nov 8 1950 was indeed the
  Wednesday after the Tuesday election. Visually confirmed:
  `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1950_masthead_crop.png`.

## Verdict up front

**CEILING = 152,068 ballots** in the governor's contest (Warren 100,482 + Roosevelt
51,586), 872 of San Diego County's 1,052 precincts (82.9%). **NOT a night count**:
`NCT19501108` is a routine **afternoon** edition that went to press after 2:30 p.m.
Wednesday, and the county figure carries no night/early-morning clock phrase. Share is
**NULL**, `comparable: false`. The true election-night plateau was lower by an unknown
amount.

## The special-edition hunt (the highest-yield move) — came up EMPTY

Both of the routes that produced the 1936 and 1940 wins were tested and both fail here:

1. **No separately-cataloged second edition.** The 1936 win came from `NCT19361104-02`
   ("EXTRA EXTRA"). For 1950 there is no `-02` sibling: fetching
   `https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=NCT19501108-02` returns CDNC's error page —
   *"Oops! Invalid value \"NCT19501108-02\" for CGI argument \"d\"."* (saved:
   `/Users/sbuss/.claude/jobs/8f24b289/tmp/sd/nct19501108_02.html`). Every date-scoped
   full-text search for 1950-11-08 (`San Diego county`, `precincts`, `Warren`) returns hits
   only under section OIDs of the form `NCT19501108.1.N` — never a `-02` sibling.
2. **No special/extra self-congratulation anywhere in the issue.** The 1940 win came from
   the paper's own editorial ("THE TIMES-ADVOCATE scooped the universe... an election
   special Wednesday morning"). For 1950: `search:"extra edition"` = **0 hits**,
   `search:"special edition"` = **0 hits** in this issue. The paper's own election-night
   colour piece ('Round Town With the PROWLER, page 1) *brags about nothing of the kind* —
   it complains instead about the fog and reports that "**the last local returns came in
   after 6 a.m. Wednesday**", i.e. the local count was still trickling in at dawn, and the
   paper simply waited and printed in the afternoon as usual.
3. **Month calendar checked.** CDNC lists exactly one `NCT` issue for 8 Nov 1950 (calendar
   fetch `CL2.1950.11&sp=NCT`, saved to
   `/Users/sbuss/.claude/jobs/8f24b289/tmp/sd/nct_195011.html`).

## Edition-timing evidence (the decisive gate) — FAILS the night test, three ways

All three are on page 1 of `NCT19501108` and are visible in
`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1950_p1_full.png`:

1. **The paper printed 2:30 p.m. wire flashes.** A boxed page-1 column reads, verbatim
   (vision-read from `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1950_latewire_crop.png`, digit-legible):
   > "**LATE WIRE BULLETINS** (From United Press Teletype) — **2:30 p.m. Flashes**"
   followed by a Los Angeles UP item about a CIO strike. An issue that carries 2:30 p.m.
   wire flashes went to press **after 2:30 p.m. on Wednesday, November 8** — roughly
   eighteen and a half hours after polls closed. This alone is dispositive.
2. **Afternoon delivery boilerplate, page-1 ear:** "IF YOU DON'T RECEIVE YOUR PAPER BY
   **5:45 O'CLOCK** PLEASE PHONE 888 / Ask for Circulation Department And One will be
   Delivered to You" — the same afternoon-delivery notice the 1952 and 1960 probes found
   (`sd-local-papers-probe.md`, Part 2). By 1948 the Times-Advocate's routine schedule had
   shifted from evening to afternoon publication and it stayed there.
3. **The returns' own clock phrases are all Wednesday daytime**, never night:
   - "**The tabulation Wednesday** of 14,368 precincts of the state's 18,048 gave for
     governor: Warren 1,475,886 and Roosevelt 840,111."
   - "**In mid-morning Wednesday**, Gehres sent the following telegram to McKinnon..."
   - Page-1 weather box: "**T-A Thermometer Reading At Noon: 72.**" — a noon-Wednesday
     temperature reading, printed in this issue.
   - Want-ad boilerplate (page 6): "Deadline for publication of Want Ads same day as placed
     is **11 a.m.**"

   The only night-clocked sentence in the whole issue is about Warren's *statement*, not the
   count: "Governor Warren acknowledged reelection... with a short victory statement issued
   shortly before 1 a.m." That is a concession-timing fact, not a returns timestamp.

Per `README.md`'s morning/afternoon trap and the `1932-11-08.md` CORRECTION: a routine
afternoon paper's day-after issue is a **next-day CEILING**, never a night count, even when
it prints exact digits.

| Contest | Candidate | Votes |
|---|---|---|
| Governor | Earl Warren (R) | **100,482** |
| Governor | James Roosevelt (D) | **51,586** |

**Contest sum = 100,482 + 51,586 = 152,068 ballots**, 872 of San Diego County's 1,052
precincts (82.9%). Only two candidates are printed; no minor-party governor line appears in

Not summed (out of scope, recorded for context only): U.S. Senate (Nixon 89,734 / Douglas

## Independent corroboration — Oceanside Blade-Tribune, same day, same digits

`BT19501108` (Oceanside Blade-Tribune, also an evening/afternoon paper — its 1932 masthead,
quoted in `1932-11-08.md`, reads "Published Every Evening Except Sundays and Holidays")
carries a boxed item headed "**SAN DIEGO COUNTY VOTING RECORD**":

> "San Diego county totals on governor and attorney general race, with **872 out of 1052
> precincts complete**: Warren **100,482**, Roosevelt **51,586**. Shattuck 109,466, Brown
> 30,207."
> (OCR: `mirror/cdnc/BT19501108/search_Warren.txt`)

Identical governor digits and identical precinct basis, from an independent paper — strong
confirmation of the transcription. (The Blade-Tribune's *attorney-general* digits differ
from the Times-Advocate's; irrelevant to the target contest, and at least one of the two is
an OCR or typesetting error.) It does **not** rescue the timing: `BT19501108` is likewise an
afternoon paper, and its own page carries "Dow Jones **2 p.m. EST** stock averages"
(= 11 a.m. Pacific) — so its returns, too, are a Wednesday-daytime snapshot. Notably its
STATEWIDE governor figure (Warren 1,415,102) is *lower* than the Times-Advocate's
(1,475,886), i.e. the Blade-Tribune went to press slightly earlier — yet both print the
same 872/1,052 county numbers, showing the county figure is one fixed United Press
compilation reproduced by both, not each paper's own night desk.

## Verdict

**CEILING = 152,068 ballots** (Warren 100,482 + Roosevelt 51,586), 872 of 1,052 San Diego
County precincts (82.9%), per a United Press county compilation printed in the Escondido
Daily Times-Advocate's routine **afternoon** edition of Wednesday 1950-11-08 (press time
after 2:30 p.m.) and reproduced in the Oceanside Blade-Tribune the same day.

- **share: NULL**, `comparable: false` — this is a next-day ceiling, ~18 hours after polls
  closed, not the election-night plateau. Per RUNBOOK 5.2 it may be carried only as a
  documented ceiling.
- **Ceiling as a share of certified** (now that the denominator is landed): 152,068 / 190,933
  = **≤ 79.6%** of the certified governor contest sum (or ≤ 78.7% of the SOV's printed
  193,262 total ballots cast). This is an upper bound on the election-night share, NOT the
  share itself: the true night plateau was lower by an unknown amount.
- `ap-remote-basis`: **NO** — these are San Diego County's own local papers, not a remote
  wire pickup.
- confidence: secondary (transcription is solid and doubly sourced; only the timing
  disqualifies it).
- Denominator gate: **PASS**. The certified figure is now landed in `denominators.md`
  (§ 1950-11-07 — Governor): San Diego certified governor contest sum **190,933** (Warren
  128,309 / Roosevelt 62,598 / Scattering 26), CA SoS *Statement of Vote, General Election
  November 7, 1950*, printed p.4, archive.org `stateofcaliforn194050cali` leaf n497
  (https://archive.org/download/stateofcaliforn194050cali/page/n497_w1400.jpg). The floor
  152,068 sits comfortably under it, so the transcription is arithmetically admissible; the
  ceiling can now be stated as ≤ 79.6%, but still never as a night share.
- Bonus corroboration from the same SoV: printed p.3 (leaf n496) gives San Diego County
  **1,052 voting precincts** — exactly the precinct denominator the Times-Advocate and
  Blade-Tribune print ("872 of the county's 1,052"). The certified record independently
  confirms the papers' precinct basis.
