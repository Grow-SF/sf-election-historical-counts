## Numerator: the printed county compilation (target contest, PRESIDENT)

Verbatim, article `SDDU19201103.2.11` (vision-read from `_zsd6.png` / `_crop_pres2.png`):

> "The vote for president and vice president in this county in 73 city and county
> precincts, out of 223, gives:
> Harding and Coolidge......567[8?]
> Cox and Roosevelt.........250[0?]"

| Contest | Ticket | Votes | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| President | Harding-Coolidge (R) | **5,678** | last digit reads 8 (vs 3), flagged |
| President | Cox-Roosevelt (D) | **2,500** | last digit reads 0 (vs 9), flagged |

No Debs or Watkins county figures are printed anywhere in the issue's SD returns.

**Contest sum = 5,678 + 2,500 = 8,178 ballots** counted in the presidential contest,
73 of 223 county precincts, compiled by the Union's own staff ("a large staff at
work last night... Numerous adding machines") by press time of the 5 o'clock EXTRA.

Same-basis contests from the same article (context):
- U.S. senator, same 73 precincts: "Shortridge 4617, Phelan 326[3?]" → sum 7,880.
- Congress 11th district (7 counties, "eighty-five out of 223" SD precincts per the
  OCR text): Swing 5[8?]16, Dickson 21[5?]1 (digits not fully verified; context only).

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 8,178 ballots** in the presidential contest (Harding-Coolidge
5,678 + Cox-Roosevelt 2,500), 73 of 223 San Diego County precincts, compiled by the
San Diego Union for its E+1 five-o'clock morning EXTRA. Only the two major tickets
were printed; Debs/Watkins votes exist but are unprinted, so the true contest count
is slightly higher — 8,178 stands as a floor on both grounds (partial precincts +
missing minor tickets). Share of certified contest total: 8,178 / 31,098 = **26.3%**
(denominator landed 2026-07-11: SOV 1920 President contest sum, `denominators.md`).
