## Numerator: "THE ENTIRE COUNTY" recap (target contest, PRESIDENT)

Verbatim, article `SDDU19041109.2.13` (vision-read from `sd_19041109_p3_hires_county6.png`):

> "THE ENTIRE COUNTY.
> Combining the figures of the city and country on presidential electors and
> congressman, the totals show as follows:
> For president—
> Roosevelt .......................... 3,79[4?]
> Parker .............................. 1,187
> Debs ................................ 1,200
> Swallow ................................ 119
> Roosevelt's plurality over Parker, 2,607; over Debs, 2,594."

| Candidate | Party | Votes | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Theodore Roosevelt | Republican | **3,794** | leading digit read as "3" (visually could pass for "5" at this scan resolution); resolved by the printed plurality lines, which are self-consistent ONLY with 3,794 (3,794-1,187=2,607 and 3,794-1,200=2,594, both matching the printed pluralities exactly); 5,794 would fail both the plurality cross-check and the certified-total gate (see below) |
| Alton B. Parker | Democrat | **1,187** | clean read, confirmed at high zoom (`_hires_parker.png`) |
| Eugene V. Debs | Socialist | **1,200** | clean read, confirmed at high zoom; also matches city (768) + country (432) sub-totals printed earlier in the same article |
| Silas C. Swallow | Prohibition | **119** | clean read |

**Contest sum = 3,794 + 1,187 + 1,200 + 119 = 6,300 ballots** counted in the president
contest, per the Union's own combined city+county compilation, printed as "the totals"
(not qualified as partial the way the 1902 recap was) but NOT fully complete: the same
article states "All of the precincts of the city have been heard from save one, the
sixth precinct of the First ward — Sorrento — from which it is impossible to hear
because there is no telephone in that vicinity," and for the outside precincts, "With
several precincts in the mountain districts and all but two from the desert yet to
hear from, the totals in the outside district show as follows" (preceding the country
sub-total that feeds into this combined figure). So this is a near-complete but not
100% county count.

Same-recap contests, for context (not summed here): Congress — Smith 3,590(?), Lucas
1,381(?), Richardson 1,112(?), Cloes 112(?) ("Smith's plurality over Lucas, 2,259; over
Richardson, 2,478" — roughly consistent, digits not fully re-verified since Congress
isn't the target contest).

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 6,300 ballots** in the president contest (Roosevelt 3,794 + Parker
1,187 + Debs 1,200 + Swallow 119), near-complete county returns (all-but-one city
precinct, all-but-several outside precincts) as compiled by the San Diego Union's
county desk for its Nov. 9, 1904 morning edition. Share of certified final (7,241):
**6,300 / 7,241 = 87.0%**.
