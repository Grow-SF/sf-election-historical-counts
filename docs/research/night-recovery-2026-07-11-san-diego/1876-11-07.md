## Numerator: "ELECTION DAY" precinct compilation (target contest, PRESIDENT)

Every digit below was read directly from page-image crops (never from OCR
alone); all crops were legible with no ambiguous digits (bold, clean type
throughout this article — noticeably crisper than the 1872 issue).

Lead paragraph (`_electionday_top.png`), vision-read verbatim:

> "The election in San Diego yesterday passed off with the utmost quiet and
> good order under the workings of the admirable election law of California.
> The total vote polled in the five wards of the city was 609, being 23 less
> than the vote of last year."

### San Diego city, five wards

| Ward | Hayes (R) | Tilden (D) | Sum |
|---|---|---|---|
| First | 22 | 50 | 72 |
| Second | 30 | 27 | 57 |
| Third | 117 | 88 | 205 |
| Fourth | 137 | 54 | 191 |
| Fifth | 61 | 23 | 84 |
| **Printed RECAPITULATION** | **367** | **242** | **609** |

Our per-ward sum (22+30+117+137+61=367 for Hayes; 50+27+88+54+23=242 for
Tilden) matches the paper's own printed city RECAPITULATION EXACTLY on both
columns, and 367+242=609 matches the lead paragraph's "total vote polled in
the five wards ... was 609" exactly as well — full three-way cross-check,
no discrepancy at the city level (unlike 1872's small 1-3-vote per-ward
gaps).

### Outlying precincts ("Outside Precincts" section)

Lead-in, vision-read verbatim: "We have returns from the following outside
precincts: National, Monument, Central, Campo, and San Luis Rey, aggregating
a vote of 262, making a total vote so far as heard from of 871 — a little
more than half of the estimated vote of the county."

| Precinct | Hayes (R) | Tilden (D) | Sum |
|---|---|---|---|
| National City | 39 | 9 | 48 |
| Monument | 19 | 12 | 31 |
| Central | 45 | 22 | 67 |
| Campo | 22 | 29 | 51 |
| San Luis Rey | 41 | 25 | 66 |
| **Sum of the 5 precincts above** | **166** | **97** | **263** |
| **Printed outside-precincts RECAPITULATION** | **167** | **97** | — |

Our Tilden outside-precinct sum (97) matches the printed recapitulation
exactly; our Hayes sum (166) is 1 vote below the printed recapitulation
(167) and our sum-of-sums (263) is 1 above the paper's stated "aggregating a
vote of 262" — small period compositor/arithmetic noise of the same order
seen in the 1872 dossier, not a transcription ambiguity (every individual
digit crop was crisp).

### "TOTAL RESULT TO DATE" (paper's own grand total, printed)

> "In city and county, as far as heard from: Hayes, 532; Tilden, 339.
> Pacheco, 541; Wigginton, 321. Majority for Hayes, 193; for Pacheco, 220."

This line is doubly self-consistent: (1) 532 − 339 = 193, matching both the
printed "Majority for Hayes, 193" AND the article's own headline ("Hayes 193
... Ahead"); (2) 532 + 339 = 871, matching the separately-stated "total vote
so far as heard from of 871" in the Outside Precincts lead-in — two
independently-printed sentences corroborate the same grand total. Because
this printed total is corroborated twice while our own ward/precinct-level
resummation carries a small (~2-vote) drift typical of hand-set 19th-century
tables, **the printed "TOTAL RESULT TO DATE" is used as the contest sum**,
per the same "faithful to what's printed" principle as the 1872 and 1884
dossiers — this is not a case of guessing a garbled digit; it is choosing
between two internally-consistent printed totals, and only this one is
corroborated by an independent sentence.

**Contest sum = 532 (Hayes) + 339 (Tilden) = 871 ballots** in the
presidential contest, covering San Diego city (5 wards, 609 votes) plus 5
named outlying precincts (National City, Monument, Central, Campo, San Luis
Rey; 262 votes) — the same set of precincts as the 1872-11-05 dossier, plus
Monument (not reported in 1872).

## Precincts-reported basis

No "X of Y county precincts" figure is printed. The Outside Precincts
paragraph explicitly self-labels this as partial: "a little more than half
of the estimated vote of the county" — i.e., the paper itself estimates the
871 printed here is roughly HALF of San Diego County's expected total vote,
making this explicitly and quantifiably a floor (unlike 1872, where the
"more returns needed" language was qualitative only). Precincts-reported
ratio: **PENDING** — no named county precinct-count denominator, but the
paper's own "little more than half" estimate is a useful qualitative anchor.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 871 ballots** in the presidential contest (Hayes 532 +
Tilden 339), covering San Diego city (5 wards, 609 votes, exact 3-way
cross-check) plus 5 outlying precincts (National City, Monument, Central,
Campo, San Luis Rey; 262 votes), printed in the San Diego Union's Wednesday
1876-11-08 morning edition, using the paper's own doubly-corroborated "TOTAL
RESULT TO DATE" line. The paper itself estimates this is "a little more than
half of the estimated vote of the county," making 871 an explicit,
paper-quantified floor rather than merely an inferred one. Share of certified
final: **59.6%** (871 / 1,462 certified; certified figure from
`denominators.md` 1876-11-07 entry, Tribune Almanac for 1877 p.65).
