## Numerator: two DIFFERENT totals printed in the same article — reconciled below

### (a) The paper's own opening "totals of these returns" summary

*"For Governor—Phelps, 238; Irwin, 303; Bidwell, 125. Plurality for Irwin,
55."* This paragraph explicitly claims to be the sum of "full returns from
the five Wards of the City, and from Campo, Central and Monument precincts."
**Internal arithmetic problem: 303 − 238 = 65, not the printed plurality of
55** — i.e. this summary paragraph contains its own printed arithmetic
error, independent of anything read from the detailed table below.

### (b) Our own sum of the printed precinct-by-precinct table

| Precinct | Phelps (R) | Irwin (D) | Bidwell (Ind) | Sum |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| First Ward (Old Town) | 14 | 50 | 5 | 69 |
| Second Ward | 27 | 20 | 9 | 56 |
| Third Ward | 94 | 83 | 24 | 201 |
| Fourth Ward | 117 | 65 | 34 | 216 |
| Fifth Ward | 35 | 20 | 26 | 81 |
| Monument | 16 | 13 | 13 | 42 |
| Central | 23 | 18 | 14 | 55 |
| **Column totals (7 precincts)** | **326** | **269** | **125** | **720** |
| Campo (telegraphic, majorities only — see below) | not printed | not printed | not printed | excluded |

Every ward's Governor line was read directly from its own page-image crop;
Third and Fourth Wards (the two largest) were each independently re-cropped
and re-read a second time with an identical result both times, so
transcription error in those two lines is unlikely.

### Reconciling (a) and (b)

**Bidwell's total matches exactly between the two methods (125 = 125)**,
which is strong evidence the precinct-table transcription is accurate (a
coincidental exact match across 7 independently-read lines summing to a
number that then exactly equals the paper's own printed total would be very
unlikely by chance). Phelps and Irwin, however, do **not** reconcile:
the precinct-table sum gives Phelps 326 / Irwin 269 (Phelps ahead by 57),
while the opening summary gives Phelps 238 / Irwin 303 (Irwin ahead by 65,
itself misprinted as 65-and-labeled-55 — see above). Because (1) the summary
paragraph is independently known to contain an arithmetic error (55 vs. the
correct 65), (2) the precinct-table reads for the two largest wards were
each confirmed twice, and (3) Bidwell's independent exact match validates
the transcription method, this dossier treats the **precinct-table sum
(720) as the more reliable numerator** and records the opening-paragraph
total (666) as a flagged, unresolved discrepancy rather than silently
preferring one number — see Human-verification asks below. Plausible
explanation (not verified): the opening summary was telegraphed/set in type
from an earlier partial count before the full Third and Fourth Ward returns
(the two largest, and the ones most responsible for the gap) were tallied,
and the paper never reconciled its lead paragraph against the fuller table
printed immediately below it in the same article — a known pattern in
election-night rush composition.

**Contest sum (numerator used) = 720 ballots** (Phelps 326 + Irwin 269 +
Bidwell 125) across the 7 precincts with full candidate-level Governor
returns printed (First through Fifth Wards of San Diego City, Monument, and
Central). Campo precinct is **excluded** from this sum: its report (received
"By Telegraph") gives only the WINNING margin per office ("majorities:
Irwin, 23; ... Total vote polled, 47"), not each candidate's raw vote count,
so it cannot be added to a per-candidate sum without fabricating a split.

## Precincts-reported basis

8 of the county's precincts are covered by this article (5 city Wards +
Monument + Central + Campo), explicitly described by the paper as "more
than half of the aggregate vote of the County." Of these, 7 have full
per-candidate Governor figures (used in the 720 sum) and 1 (Campo) has only
majority/total-vote figures (excluded). The article's closing line — "We
expect returns from all the precincts except Aguanga, Agua Caliente and San
Jacinto before our next issue when we shall publish them in tabular form" —
confirms additional county precincts (the mining district towns) remained
outstanding at press time. 720 is therefore a genuine night-count floor:
more than half the county by the paper's own estimate, with named precincts
still to come. Precincts-reported ratio: **PENDING** — no total county
precinct count found in this issue.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 720 ballots** in the Governor contest (Phelps 326 +
Irwin 269 + Bidwell 125), summed from the 7 San Diego County precincts with
full candidate-level returns printed in the San Diego Union's Thursday
1875-09-02 morning edition (First through Fifth Wards of San Diego City,
Monument, and Central) — under the headline "VICTORY! ... A SWEEPING
TRIUMPH! Full Returns from the City, Monument, Central and Campo
Precincts." This numerator EXCLUDES Campo precinct (telegraphic report,
majorities only, no per-candidate counts) and explicitly named outstanding
precincts (Aguanga, Agua Caliente, San Jacinto, and others not yet in). The
paper's own opening-paragraph summary total (666) does **not** match this
precinct-table sum and is flagged rather than trusted, because it also
contains an internal arithmetic error in its own printed plurality. Share of
certified final: **45.0%** (720 / 1,600 certified; certified figure from
`denominators.md` 1875-09-01 entry, Tribune Almanac for 1877 p.65).
