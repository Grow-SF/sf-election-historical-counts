# 1872-11-05 presidential general — San Diego County night count (San Diego Union, 1872-11-06)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1872-11-05, presidential general (Grant-Wilson R, Greeley-
  Brown D/Liberal-R, O'Conor-Adams Straight-Out Democrat on the California
  ballot). San Diego was a very small town in 1872 (pre-boom, five wards) plus
  a handful of outlying county precincts reachable by telegraph.
- Calendar check: `https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=cl&cl=CL2.1872.11&sp=SDDU&e=...`
  lists `SDDU18721105` through `SDDU18721130` (gaps only on Sundays/holidays);
  `SDDU18721106` (the expected day-after issue) exists — no gap to document.
- Day-after issue: San Diego Union and Daily Bee, Wednesday morning 1872-11-06,
  Volume 4, Number 510. Issue OID `SDDU18721106` (42 sections). Issue URL:
  https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=SDDU18721106
- Masthead gate: PASS — running head on page 3 reads "THE DAILY UNION.
  Wednesday, ... November 6, 1872" (`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/sd_18721106_p3.png`,
  full page); CDNC's own issue title independently gives "6 November 1872,
  Volume 4, Number 510." Nov 5 1872 was a Tuesday, so Nov 6 was correctly a
  Wednesday.

## Numerator: ward-by-ward + outlying-precinct President contest

Every figure below was read directly from page-image crops (never from OCR
alone).

### San Diego city, five wards ("FULL RETURNS FROM ALL THE WARDS")

| Ward | Grant-Wilson (R) | Greeley-Brown (D/LR) | O'Conor-Adams (SO-D) | Printed "Total vote polled" | Sum of candidates |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| First | 35 | 39 | — | 75 | 74 |
| Second | 33 | 20 | 2 | 55 | 55 |
| Third | 103 | 69 | 1 | 174 | 173 |
| Fourth | 80 | 41 | 1 | 124 | 122 |
| Fifth | 38 | 3 | — | 42 | 41 |
| **Column totals** | **289** | **172** | **4** | **470** | **465** |

Small 1-3 vote gaps between "total vote polled" (a distinct hand-tally line
covering ALL that ward's races) and the President-only candidate sum are
recorded faithfully as printed, consistent with the 1884 dossier's finding
that these two lines are independent tallies, not required to match exactly
(down-ballot blanks / scratched ballots).

### RECAPITULATION (city-wide, printed by the paper itself)

> "Total number of votes cast in the city, 470. For Grant and Wilson, 289;
> Greeley and Brown, 172; O'Conor and Adams, 4. Majority for Grant, 117. For
> Houghton, 313; for Kewen, 152. Majority for Houghton, 161. For aid to the
> San Diego and Los Angeles Railroad, 348; against aid, 43. Majority for aid
> to the Railroad, 305."

This printed recapitulation (289 + 172 + 4 = 465) matches the sum of the five
per-ward President lines EXACTLY, both column-by-column and in total — a
strong internal cross-check that the ward digits were read correctly.

### Outlying precincts (telegraphed dispatches, printed as separate articles)

| Precinct | Grant-Wilson (R) | Greeley-Brown (D/LR) | Sum |
|---|---|---|---|
| National City ("Twenty Majority for Grant") | 25 | 5 | 30 |
| San Luis Rey ("Five Majority for Greeley") | 18 | 23 | 41 |

National City text verbatim: "The election at National City passed off very
quietly yesterday. Following are the returns: Total vote polled, 35.
Presidential Ticket — Grant and Wilson, 25; Greeley and Brown, 5. Majority
for Grant and Wilson, 20. Congressional Ticket — Houghton, 27; Kewen, 8.
Majority for Houghton, 19. [Aid] to S.D. and L.A.R.R — For aid, [?]; against
aid, 27. Majority against, 25." (President sum 30 vs. "total vote polled 35"
— a 5-vote gap, same pattern as the city wards.)

San Luis Rey text verbatim (telegraphed, "By Telegraph to the Union"): "SAN
LUIS REY, Nov. 5. — This precinct gives the following vote: For Grant and
Wilson, 18; Greeley and Brown, 23. [For] Houghton, 31; Kewen, 14. For aid to
the San Diego and Los Angeles Railroad, 30." No "total vote polled" or
"against aid" figure is printed for this precinct — recorded exactly as
printed.

### Contest sum across all reported precincts

**465 (city, 5 wards) + 30 (National City) + 41 (San Luis Rey) = 536 ballots**
in the presidential contest: Grant-Wilson 289+25+18 = **332**; Greeley-Brown
172+5+23 = **200**; O'Conor-Adams 4+0+0 = **4**. 332+200+4 = 536, checks.

## Precincts-reported basis

No "X of Y county precincts" figure is printed anywhere in this issue's
returns. Eight named units are covered (5 San Diego city wards + National
City + San Luis Rey, though the wards are reported as one recapitulated city
total): the article headline claims only "FULL RETURNS FROM ALL THE WARDS"
(i.e., complete for the city), and National City/San Luis Rey are explicitly
labeled as telegraphed dispatches from individual outlying precincts — no
language here (unlike the 1884 dossier's "a great deal of scratching...other
returns will be needed") signals that county precincts are still missing, but
1872 San Diego County certainly had other settlements (e.g., Old Town/environs
may be folded into "San Diego" wards; other county precincts are not named).

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 536 ballots** in the presidential contest (Grant-Wilson
332 + Greeley-Brown 200 + O'Conor-Adams 4), across San Diego city (5 wards,
465 votes, cross-checked against the paper's own RECAPITULATION) plus two
outlying precincts reported by telegraph (National City 30, San Luis Rey 41),
printed in the San Diego Union's Wednesday 1872-11-06 morning edition. The
paper explicitly declares the city return "FULL" (all five wards complete and
official) but does not claim county-wide completeness, and no other outlying
precincts are named in this issue — so 536 should be treated as a floor for
the city + these two telegraphed precincts specifically, not necessarily all
of San Diego County. Share of certified final: **61.4%** (536 / 873
certified; certified figure from `denominators.md` 1872-11-05 entry,
Tribune Almanac for 1877 p.65).
