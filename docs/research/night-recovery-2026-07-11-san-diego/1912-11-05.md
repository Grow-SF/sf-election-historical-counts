## Verdict

**Night count (contest floor): 17,566 ballots counted in the PRESIDENT contest**
(Chafin 914 + Debs 2,044 + Wilson 8,103 + Roosevelt 6,505, the table's printed
"Grand totals" row) across **114 precinct rows** (68 of 73 city precincts + 46
county precincts), "received up to 2 o'clock this morning", printed in the
day-after San Diego Union of Wednesday 1912-11-06, page 6.

- My independent per-row transcription sums: Chafin 914 (exact match), Debs
  2,044 (exact), Wilson 8,103 (exact), Roosevelt 6,417 (-88 vs printed 6,505;
  two 3-vs-8 digit ambiguities flagged below). Congress columns of the same
  totals row (Evans 3,426 / Kettner 10,545) are independently CONFIRMED verbatim
  by the p1 Kettner story ("San Diego county's vote is 10,545 for 'Bruder' to
  3,426 for Evans"), which validates the flat-top-3 typeface reads.
- Gate: masthead reads "THE SAN DIEGO UNION: WEDNESDAY MORNING, NOVEMBER 6,
  1912" == expected E+1 issue. PASS.
- Gate: contest sum <= certified county total for President: **PASS**.
  Certified SD County contest sum (SoV highest-elector, `denominators.md`) =
  21,728 (Wilson 9,731 + Roosevelt 7,922 + Debs 2,873 + Chafin 1,139 + Taft
  write-in electors 63). 17,566 <= 21,728. Every candidate column also passes
  individually (8,103<=9,731; 6,505<=7,922; 2,044<=2,873; 914<=1,139).
- Gate: precincts-reported ratio: 114 rows printed; the table itself says it
  covers only precincts reported by 2 a.m. (city precincts First, Fourth,
  Fifth, Seventh and Seventy-first are absent from the table). Ratio < 1. PASS.

Arithmetic: printed President contest total = 914 + 2,044 + 8,103 + 6,505 =
**17,566** counted ballots (floor). My row sums give 17,478 (-0.50%); the whole
gap sits in the Roosevelt column (-88), see flags. Congress sum (3,426 +
10,545 = 13,971) and Judge sum (6,793 + 8,298 = 15,091) are both below the
President sum, consistent with normal roll-off (no gate implication).

Notes on the printed table:
- City precincts First, Fourth, Fifth, Seventh, Seventy-first are NOT in the
  table (not yet reported by 2 a.m.); all other city precincts 2-73 print.
- Seventeenth Precinct prints 46/57/152/147 for President but 0/0 and 0/0 for
  Congress/Judge - as printed.
- Taft is not a column: the 1912 CA ballot carried no Taft electors (the
  Roosevelt electors took the Republican line's place); the paper prints
  exactly the four elector slates that were on the California ballot.

  (small closed loop, hooked ascender) and third digit a clear "0" (clean
  closed oval) — both confirmed against a directly adjacent, unambiguous "1"
  (Wilson's "8103", thin vertical stroke, visibly distinct shape) and "0"

  slot — the printed digit is unmistakably round, not a stroke.
  - **CORRECTS the framing, not the number**: the -88 gap is confirmed to be a
    real discrepancy between the printed Grand-totals row and the dossier's own
    hand-summed precinct rows, not a misreading of "6505" as "6417" — those are
    genuinely two different sequences, and the printed one is what's used.

  scan's resolution — both render as blurry rounded blobs under further

  reference digits: neither shows the double-closed-loop figure-eight profile
