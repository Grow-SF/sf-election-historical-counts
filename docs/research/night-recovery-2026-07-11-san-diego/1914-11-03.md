# 1914-11-03 gubernatorial general — San Diego County night count (Sacramento Union AP flash, 1914-11-04)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1914-11-03, Governor. Candidates on the CA ballot: Hiram W. Johnson
  (Progressive, incumbent), John D. Fredericks (Republican), J. B. Curtin (Democratic),
  Noble A. Richardson (Socialist), Clinton P. Moore (Prohibition).
- Target contest: Governor (the single-seat statewide contest of 1914).
- Route: the San Diego Union (`SDDU`) is **dark for November 1914** — CDNC returns zero
  `SDDU` issues for the whole month (re-verified this session against the Nov-1914
  calendar, `sp=SDDU`, empty). Recovery therefore went the 1924/1928 route: an AP/wire
  San Diego county line printed the morning after in **another** California morning paper.

## Verdict (short)

**NIGHT COUNT (floor) = 3,543 ballots** in the Governor contest — Fredericks 1,878 +
Johnson 1,665 — at **20 of San Diego city-and-county's 178 precincts (11.2%)**, per the
Sacramento Daily Union's election-night wire dispatch (dateline "SAN DIEGO, Nov. 3"),
printed in its **Wednesday MORNING, November 4, 1914** edition.

**Share of certified contest total: 3,543 / 34,955 = 10.1%.**

Two caveats that dim this figure and must travel with it:

1. `ap-remote-basis` — this is a Sacramento paper's wire snapshot, not San Diego's own
   compilation. Per the campaign's own 1920 cross-check (AP/remote 40/223 precincts vs.
   the SD Union's own 73/223 the same night), a remote wire flash badly lags what the
   county's own press had. This is a **dimmed lower bound**, not a clean night count.
2. `partial-candidate-sum` — the dispatch prints only **two of the six** ballot-listed
   gubernatorial candidates for San Diego (Fredericks and Johnson; no Curtin, Richardson,
   Moore, or scattering). The contest sum is therefore a floor of a floor: the true
   20-precinct ballot count was higher by however many Curtin/Richardson/Moore votes those
   20 precincts cast (countywide those three took 6,437 of 34,955 = 18.4% of the contest,
   so the 20-precinct figure is plausibly understated by roughly a fifth).

## Numerator: the printed San Diego line

Verbatim, vision-read from `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/sdu1914_sd_crop.png` (page 3,
continuation of `SDU19141104.2.2`):

> **FREDERICKS LEADS IN SAN DIEGO.**
> SAN DIEGO, Nov. 3.—Twenty out of 178 precincts in the city and county gave for
> governor: Fredericks, 1878; Johnson, 1665.
> Senator—Eleven precincts: Knowland, 343; Phelan, 195; Heney, 408.

| Contest | Candidate | Votes printed |
|---|---|---|
| Governor | John D. Fredericks (R) | **1,878** |
| Governor | Hiram W. Johnson (Prog) | **1,665** |
| Governor | J. B. Curtin (D) | *not printed* |
| Governor | Noble A. Richardson (Soc) | *not printed* |
| Governor | Clinton P. Moore (Proh) | *not printed* |

**Contest sum (Governor, as printed) = 1,878 + 1,665 = 3,543 ballots**, 20 of 178 San
Diego city-and-county precincts.

The Senate line in the same paragraph is on a **different** precinct basis (11 precincts,
not 20), so it is not a cross-check on the governor total and is recorded here only for
completeness. It does explain an apparent inconsistency that first showed up in the raw
OCR: the governor line averages 177 votes/precinct while the senate line averages 86 —
because they are 20-precinct and 11-precinct snapshots respectively, not the same
precincts.

## Cross-check: an independent morning paper's own San Diego flash

**Riverside Morning Enterprise** (`MPE`), issue `MPE19141104`, section `MPE19141104.2.29`
(OCR: `mirror/cdnc/MPE19141104/search_San_Diego.txt`):

> "Kettner Has Lead — SAN DIEGO, Nov. 4.—Complete in 22 precincts out of 173 show Johnson
> 1,267, Fredericks 1,150, Knowland 977, Heney 901, Phelan 583. Kettner claims 15,000 over
> Needham in San Diego county."

Governor sum there = 1,267 + 1,150 = **2,417** at 22/173 precincts — a *different* wire
compilation (different precinct denominator: 173 vs 178) and a **lower** figure, so it is
corroboration of the fact pattern (two-candidate wire flashes, tiny precinct coverage, no
Curtin) rather than a better floor. The Sacramento Union's 3,543 is the higher of the two
night-clocked figures and is therefore the one recorded.

## Precincts-reported basis

**20 of 178** San Diego city-and-county precincts (0.112 ≤ 1, gate PASS), stated in the
dispatch's own sentence — numerator and precinct basis share one printed source. The 178
figure is the paper's; the SoS does not print a 1914 per-county precinct count in the
Governor table.

## Edition-type / timing evidence (the critical gate)

1. **Morning paper.** Page-1 masthead, read from `sdu1914_p1_full.png`:
   > "SACRAMENTO, CALIFORNIA, **WEDNESDAY MORNING**, NOVEMBER 4, 1914 — TWELVE PAGES."
   Same gate the 1924 dossier used for this paper. A morning paper's day-after issue goes
   to press in the small hours and captures the election-night plateau.
2. **Returns clocked to election night.** The San Diego paragraph carries the dateline
   **"SAN DIEGO, Nov. 3"** — i.e. filed on election night itself, before midnight, not
   "this afternoon". The surrounding column is explicit about its own clock: "SAN
   FRANCISCO, Nov. 3.—Scattering returns received **up to midnight** from all parts of the
   state…", and the Johnson statement is timed "At 10:30 tonight". So both conditions of
   the campaign's night-count rule hold: morning edition **and** night-clocked returns.

No special/extra edition exists to hunt for here: the Sacramento Union's own night-clocked
regular morning edition already satisfies the gate. A search of the Nov-4 CDNC calendar
found no second (`-02`) issue OID for `SDU` that day.

Cross-checks: (a) statewide totals row on the same page — Fredericks 271,990 / Johnson
460,495 / Curtin 116,121 / Richardson 50,716 / Moore 27,345 / scattering 22 — matches the
historical 1914 statewide result exactly; (b) Wikipedia's independently-sourced 1914

printed p. 6 (leaf n69), "TOTAL REGISTRATION BY COUNTIES, 1914" gives San Diego **42,753**
registered, so a 34,955 contest total is 81.8% of registration — plausible, and it
confirms the county's large 1912→1914 growth (1912 total cast was 22,933) rather than an
OCR artifact.
