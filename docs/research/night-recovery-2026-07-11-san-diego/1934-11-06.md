# 1934-11-06 gubernatorial general — San Diego County night count: NOT AVAILABLE (full county); partial local figures found

## What was found — no full-county candidate table this year

Unlike 1932 (which had a clean "Unofficial returns from 339 precincts out
of 384 in San Diego city and county" box with full candidate figures), this
issue's county-wide reporting SPLITS into two incompatible pieces, neither
of which alone gives a usable county floor:

1. **"COUNTY GOES REPUBLICAN"** (front page, screenshots
   `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/bt1934_countygop.png` /
   `bt1934_countygop2.png`): "SAN DIEGO, Nov. 7. — With the exception of one
   office, San Diego county remains solidly Republican by returns from 240
   of the 391 precincts of San Diego city and county." This gives a
   PRECINCT BASIS (240/391 = 61%) but NO vote counts for governor — only
   prose ("County voters registered majorities for Governor Merriam and his
   running mate...") and hard numbers for one down-ballot assembly race
   (Richie 10,048, Head 9,424) and for Proposition 2 (county-wide: 27,512
   yes / 16,406 no, from the SAME 240 precincts, per the "COUNTY FAVORS
   PROPOSITION 2" item).
2. **"How Local Precincts Voted"** (front page grid, screenshot
   `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/bt1934_localprecincts.png`): gives full
   candidate-level numbers, but ONLY for Oceanside's 6 precincts + Carlsbad's
   3 precincts (9 of the county's 391 precincts — a 2.3% sliver), not the
   county as a whole:

   | Ticket | Oceanside | Carlsbad | Combined |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | Merriam (R) | 795 | 413 | 1,208 |
   | Sinclair (D/EPIC) | 490 | 412 | 902 |
   | Haight (Prog.) | 199 | 75 | 274 |

   (Sum of these three = 2,384 ballots — recorded here for completeness,
   NOT used as the wave's headline floor: a 9-of-391-precinct, single-town
   subset is too thin a basis to represent "San Diego County," and reporting
   it as the county floor would be misleading next to 1932's 339/384 figure
   from the same paper.)

3. **Statewide-only figures** also appear twice on the front page
   ("ACTING GOVERNOR'S PLURALITY BOOSTED..." — Merriam 894,290, Sinclair
   689,467, Haight [illegible], from 8,883 of the state's ~10,621
   precincts; and a second AP snapshot, 7,258 of 10,721 precincts, Merriam
   661,396 / Sinclair 522,485 / Haight 148,134) — both are CALIFORNIA
   totals, not San Diego County.

`search:"San Diego"` (4 hits, pages `.1.1`–`.1.4`) was exhaustively checked;
no other section of this issue prints a full-county candidate table.

## ADDENDUM (2026-07-12): NCT/Times-Advocate re-probe finds a county-wide
## GOVERNOR table — but it gates as a CEILING, not a night count

Per `sd-local-papers-probe.md`'s recommendation, re-probed the Escondido
Daily Times-Advocate under CDNC code `NCT` (not `TAD`, which the original
1934 dossier above found inaccessible) for the same day-after issue,
`NCT19341107`.

### Access note
The bare TOC fetch (`node cdnc_fetch.js NCT19341107`) returned "0 sections"
on three attempts with 20s backoff — but `search:"the"` scoped to the same
issue/date returned real hits (`NCT19341107.1.1`, `.1.2`), proving the issue
is fully digitized and the TOC parser (not the underlying CDNC content) was
what failed. Full front page confirmed via `cdnc_shot.js` screenshot
(`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1934_p1_full.png`, "North County Times, Volume
23, Number 66, 7 November 1934" / masthead "DAILY TIMES-ADVOCATE ...
Escondido, California, Wednesday, November 7, 1934 ... Volume XXIII, Number
66" — no "EXTRA" marking anywhere on the page).

### Edition timing (the decisive check, per README's morning/afternoon trap)
- **Routine schedule**: page-2 masthead boilerplate reads "Published every
  evening, except Sunday, by PERCY EVANS, at 237 East Grand Avenue,
  Escondido, California" — the Times-Advocate's ROUTINE edition type in 1934
  was **EVENING**, same as later years.
- **This specific issue's own account of itself** (front page, zoom-verified
  at `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1934_gov_crop2.png`): "Candidates for
  state, county and district offices who were reported early Wednesday
  morning to be in the lead in returns, widened their margin of leads over
  rival candidates, according to late returns which have become available
  from additional precincts. However, the comparative figures are
  substantially as published in **the extra edition which was issued
  Wednesday morning by the Times-Advocate**. Reports 'dribbled' into the
  Times-Advocate **Wednesday afternoon**, with several precincts still to be
  heard from." This explicitly names a SEPARATE, earlier document — a
  rushed morning extra — and says today's regular numbered edition (Vol.
  XXIII No. 66, the one digitized here) contains LATER figures compiled
  from afternoon-arriving reports.
- Every specific vote table that follows repeats the afternoon clock
  explicitly: "Merriam's lead over Sinclair was reported to be about
  220,000, **Wednesday afternoon**" (statewide); "The following is the
  count **Wednesday afternoon**" (Assembly, Stream/Ludwig); "the following
  count on the supervisorial race was compiled **Wednesday afternoon**"
  (Sweet/Hurley); "authentic information was received **Wednesday
  afternoon**" (Kensington Heights/Citrus/Fallbrook precinct notes). The
  GOVERNOR table sits inside this same afternoon-clocked block, introduced
  by "Col. Ed Fletcher received more votes in San Diego county than did
  Frank F. Merriam. **The following count in 330 precincts in San Diego
  county is of interest**" — same 330/391 precinct basis as the
  explicitly-afternoon congressional and legislative tables around it.
- The morning extra itself does not appear to be separately digitized under
  CDNC — the TOC/search for `NCT19341107` surfaces only this one routine
  numbered edition; no second Nov-7 `NCT` issue or distinct OID was found.

**Verdict on timing: this is the afternoon/ceiling case (RUNBOOK 5.2), the
same trap corrected in `1932-11-08.md`.** The digitized issue is NOT the
morning extra; it is the routine evening paper, printed with reports that
had "dribbled in" all Wednesday afternoon, 12-16+ hours past the
night-count plateau.

### The county-wide GOVERNOR table (ceiling, not night count)
Zoom-verified digit-legible at
`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1934_gov_crop4.png` (crop of
`nct1934_p1_full.png`), transcription:

| Candidate | Votes |
|---|---|
| Frank F. Merriam (R) | 36,049 |
| Upton Sinclair (D/EPIC, printed "Ppton Sinclair") | 26,062 |
| Raymond L. Haight (Prog.) | 9,082 |
| **Sum** | **71,193** |

Precinct basis: 330 of San Diego county's 391 precincts (84.4%) — same base
as the congressional table two paragraphs above it (Burnham 36,874 / Izac
33,006).

### Gates
- Masthead date == expected issue date: PASS (Wednesday, November 7, 1934).
- Contest sum <= certified contest total: PASS as a ceiling. Certified 1934
  SD county Governor total (`denominators.md`) = 87,540 (Merriam 44,422 +
  Sinclair 32,073 + Haight 10,759 + Dempster 69 + Darcy 185 + Scattering
  32); the three major-candidate certified subtotal is 44,422+32,073+10,759
  = 87,254. 71,193 <= 87,254 ✓ (71,193/87,254 = 81.6% of the three-candidate
  certified total, consistent with an 84.4%-precinct partial count — no
  overshoot, no gate flag).
- Precincts-reported ratio <= 1: PASS (330/391 = 0.844).
- Clock phrase: FAILS the night-count test (afternoon-clocked, not
  night/early-morning-clocked) — per RUNBOOK 5.2 this demotes the figure
  from a night count to a documented ceiling.

### Disposition
**CEILING, not a night count** — `comparable: false`, NULL share. Ceiling =
71,193 ballots (three major candidates), <=81.6% of the three-candidate
certified total, at 330/391 precincts (84.4%), as of Wednesday afternoon
1934-11-07 (roughly 18-22 hours after polls closed at 7 or 8 p.m. Tuesday).
The true election-night plateau was lower, by an unknown amount — the
paper's own text says a genuine morning-extra edition existed with smaller
margins ("substantially as published... though the margin has widened"),
but that earlier document is not separately accessible in CDNC, so no
night-count number can be recovered from this route either.

**Overall dossier verdict remains NOT AVAILABLE as a night count** — this
addendum adds a documented ceiling (a stronger, county-wide one than the
thin 9-precinct Oceanside+Carlsbad figure above) but supersedes nothing
about the "no night count found" conclusion.
