## Key finding: no city returns printed day-after

The lead paragraph states plainly: "The average time for counting each
ticket was about four minutes, or fifteen ballots to the hour, consequently
the returns from the city are as yet incomplete. So far as received the
returns from the country [= outlying/back-country precincts] are as
follows:" — **zero San Diego city precincts are printed in this issue.**
Everything transcribed below is outlying-precinct (non-city) data. The city
returns first appear in the day+2 issue (`SDDU18921110.2.30`, "THE COUNTY
RETURNS... Total Vote In City Precincts"), which was located but not
transcribed in this pass (see Coverage note below) since the day-after issue
already yields countable returns, satisfying the assignment's precedence
rule.

## Method note: locating the ninth precinct

The raw OCR dump for section `.2.26` (fetched via `cdnc_fetch.js`) ends with
a "Justices—Foster97, Jackton 111, Yarinum 101. Constable*—McIntosh 103,
Riggs 103, Reynolds 92. VUOHU, Only partial returns hare been received from
this precinct..." fragment that does not appear in visual sequence right
after CORONADO (which fills its entire column down to the physical page
bottom). Systematic column-by-column search of page 5 (columns at
x-fractions ~0.19, 0.335, 0.51, 0.65, 0.80 of the page image) found it at the
**top of the third narrow column**, directly beneath the masthead running
head (row: "NOTHING DEFINITE | Auditor—Crenshaw... | Justices—Foster 97... |
LOCAL INTELLIGENCE | AMUSEMENTS"), i.e. printed ABOVE the "Card to the
Public" ad that begins the visible portion of that column further down page.
OCR's "VUOHU" is garbled "WILDOMAR" (a real, still-extant San Diego
back-country place name near present-day Lake Elsinore — that area was part
of San Diego County until Riverside County split off in 1893).

## Precincts-reported basis

- 9 outlying/"country" precincts printed (Otay, Spring Valley-Chollas,
  Alpine, Tia Juana, Del Mar, Chula Vista, El Cajon, Coronado, Wildomar-partial).
  The article does not print a total count of San Diego County's precincts
  for a ratio, and explicitly states more remain: "There are still a few
  precincts to be heard from, but they will not materially change the result
  here stated approximately" (from the companion piece
  `SDDU18921110.2.27`, day+2, "THE ELECTION").
- Zero city precincts printed as of the day-after issue — "the returns from
  the city are as yet incomplete."
- Day+2 (`SDDU18921110.2.30`, "THE COUNTY RETURNS... Total Vote In City
  Precincts... Recapitulation") is a substantially larger table (ward-by-ward
  city returns plus more back-country precincts) that was located (TOC'd and
  section-OCR'd) but **not transcribed** in this pass: the day-after issue
  already supplied countable returns, so per the assignment's precedence
  rule day+2 is cross-check context only, not required. Flagged here for a
  future, deeper pass if a tighter night-count floor is wanted.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 673 ballots** in the presidential contest (Harrison
347, Cleveland 209, Weaver 80, Bidwell 37), across 9 San Diego County
back-country precincts printed in the Union's 1892-11-09 morning-after
issue — 8 with complete precinct totals plus Wildomar, which the paper
itself labels a partial return. **No San Diego city precinct is printed at
all** in this issue (the paper says city counting was still in progress at
press time), so 673 is a floor on two grounds: partial back-country coverage
and zero city coverage. Share of certified final (7,712): 673 / 7,712 =
**8.7%** — a genuinely early, small fraction of the eventual count,
consistent with the article's own description of a slow hand-count under the
new Australian ballot system ("about four minutes" per ballot).
