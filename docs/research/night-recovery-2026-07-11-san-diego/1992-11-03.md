## Category scoping check

The article gives TWO absentee-remainder figures at two different scopes:
- ~40,000 uncounted absentee ballots tied specifically to the **city**
  mayoral race ("not including about 40,000 uncounted absentee ballots").
- ~94,000 uncounted absentee ballots **countywide** ("election officials...
  did not know how many of those ballots were from the city of San Diego"
  is what pins the 94,000 figure to the whole county, distinct from the
  city-only 40,000).

Crucially, the article ALSO states, for the city figure's basis: "with all
961 precincts in the city reporting and only the absentee ballots
uncounted" — an explicit statement that precinct (in-person) voting was
fully counted and absentees were the ONLY outstanding category, at least at
the city level, as of the morning-after report.

The scope mismatch: the "all precincts reporting, only absentees uncounted"
sentence is stated for the CITY (961 precincts); the 94,000 remaining figure
used for the floor is COUNTY-wide. San Diego County in 1992 had roughly 2,813
precincts total (per `denominators.md`, SOV 1992 "Precincts" column), of
which the city of San Diego's 961 are a majority but not all. There is no
separate quote confirming the REST of the county's ~1,850 precincts were also
fully reporting by the same time.

## Arithmetic

Certified total ballots cast (SoS SOV 1992, `denominators.md`): **1,002,914**.

1,002,914 − 94,000 = **908,914** → 908,914 / 1,002,914 = **90.6%** of
certified.

## Soundness argument

This is a pre-permanent-VBM era (1992): standard California practice was for
precinct (in-person) ballots to be hand/machine-counted and reported at the
precinct level on election night itself, with only mailed absentee ballots
(requiring signature verification and slower processing) lagging into the
following days. The article's own city-scoped statement — "all 961 precincts
in the city reporting and only the absentee ballots uncounted" — is direct
contemporaneous evidence that this pattern was operating on this election
night, for the largest jurisdiction in the county. Extending "precincts
done, only absentees remain" from the city to the whole county is standard
for the era and is not contradicted by anything else in the article; the
registrar's own count of 94,000 countywide absentee-ballots-remaining is
presented as the reason the "outcome may not be known" for OTHER county-wide
contests too, i.e., framed as the single remaining bottleneck, not one
category among several unaddressed ones.

This reasoning is offered explicitly per RUNBOOK's guidance that a
90-95%-band share needs an explicit argument, not silent acceptance; it is
NOT treated as airtight (see human-verification ask below), because the
"all precincts reporting" sentence is literally scoped to the city only.

## Verdict

**FLOOR = 908,914 ballots (90.6% of certified 1,002,914).** Basis: certified
total minus the article's countywide absentee-remaining figure (94,000), a
morning-after report describing the night's end state, supported by an
explicit (if city-scoped) "only absentees uncounted" statement plus the
standard pre-VBM-era county-wide practice. Confidence: `secondary`
(news-derived), and flagged as the weaker of the two floors in this batch
because of the city/county scope gap — see human ask.
