## ADDENDUM (2026-07-12): SUPERSEDED — a genuine night count recovered via
## NCT (Escondido Daily Times-Advocate)'s "EXTRA EXTRA" morning edition

Per `sd-local-papers-probe.md`'s recommendation, re-probed under CDNC code
**`NCT`** (not `TAD`, the wrong/inaccessible code the original dossier above
tried). This route succeeds cleanly and **overturns the NOT AVAILABLE
verdict**: San Diego County's own paper printed a genuine, night-clocked,
county-wide, single-seat PRESIDENTIAL contest table.

### Access note
As with the 1934 re-probe, the bare TOC fetch for `NCT19361104` returned "0
sections" (parser flake, not a real gap — confirmed by successful
`search:` hits). Searching surfaced **two distinct digitized editions**
sharing the same date: the routine numbered edition (`NCT19361104`, "Volume
XXV, Number 63") and a separately cataloged **`NCT19361104-02` ("Edition
02")** — confirmed by direct page screenshot
(`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/nct1936_ed02_p1_full.png`) to carry its own
banner masthead: **"EXTRA EXTRA / DAILY TIMES-ADVOCATE ... ROOSEVELT
WINS"** — same Volume XXV, Number 63, Wednesday, November 4, 1936, but a
visibly different, hastily-composed front page (single page only, no
further pages cataloged under `-02`).

### Edition timing (the decisive check)
- **Routine schedule**: same masthead boilerplate as 1934/1940 — "Published
  every evening except Sunday and legal holidays" (`NCT19361104.1.2`).
- **This specific morning extra is independently documented by the paper's
  own routine edition**: on the routine edition's front page
  (`NCT19361104.1.1`), under the headline "TIMES-ADVOCATE OFFICE GIVES
  ELECTION SERVICE": *"All night election service was provided its readers
  by the Times-Advocate Tuesday night and Wednesday morning. Many
  Escondidans availed themselves of the opportunity to visit the
  Times-Advocate office or to phone in order to get results of election
  contests in which they were interested. United Press reports were
  received at intervals, furnishing information for those who called in
  person or by phone and also providing lively material on the election for
  an extra edition which was distributed to subscribers free of charge
  early Wednesday morning."* This directly confirms the `-02` "EXTRA EXTRA"
  edition is exactly what it claims to be: a genuine, free, early-Wednesday
  (pre-dawn) special.
- **Internal clock phrases in the extra itself** (and in the identical wire
  copy reprinted, labeled "(Early United Press dispatches to
  Times-Advocate)," on the routine edition's page 1, continued to page 3):
  national tally "in the 6 a. m. compilation of the United Press"; "Roosevelt
  Retires at 2 A. M. ... President Roosevelt retired shortly before 2 a. m.
  today"; "Knox Extends Congratulations ... Knox said in a telegram to the
  president at 12:45 a. m."; House returns "at 1:30 a. m."; Senate returns
  "at 2 a. m., E.S.T."; "States in which Gov. Alf M. Landon was leading in
  returns up to an early hour this morning." The San Diego county paragraph
  (below) is the last item in this same continuous pre-dawn UP sequence, with
  no later re-dating — squarely a **night/early-morning-clocked** source, the
  positive case the README's morning/afternoon test is designed to pass.

Precinct basis: 167 San Diego county precincts (total county precinct count

figure is the 20th congressional district (San Diego + Imperial counties
combined), not the county total, and is not used here.

### Gates
- Masthead date == expected issue date: PASS (both copies dated Wednesday,
  November 4, 1936, Volume XXV, Number 63).
- Contest sum <= certified contest total: PASS. Certified 1936 SD county
  President total (`denominators.md`) = 101,854 (Roosevelt 64,628 + Landon
  35,686 + Prohibition 579 + Socialist 524 + Communist 437); two-candidate
  certified subtotal = 100,314. 32,370 <= 100,314 ✓.
- Precincts-reported ratio <= 1: presumed true (explicit partial-returns
  language, "Returns from 167... precincts"), but the county's 1936 total
  precinct count was not found in this issue, so the ratio itself is not
  numerically verified — flagged below.
- Clock phrase: PASSES the night-count test (pre-dawn UP wire, "EXTRA
  EXTRA" edition, distributed free early Wednesday morning per the paper's
  own account) — this is the positive case, not the 1932/1934
  afternoon-ceiling trap.

### Verdict: NIGHT COUNT (supersedes "NOT AVAILABLE")
**32,370 ballots** (Roosevelt 21,411 + Landon 10,959), **167 precincts**, from
the Escondido Daily Times-Advocate's "EXTRA EXTRA" edition
(`NCT19361104-02`), distributed free early Wednesday morning 1936-11-04,
corroborated by the identical dispatch reprinted in the routine edition's
page-3 continuation of the same wire story.

- **Share = 32,370 / 101,854 = 31.8%** of the full 5-candidate certified
  total (or 32,370 / 100,314 = 32.3% of the two-candidate certified
  subtotal — see human-verification ask #3 on which denominator convention
  this project uses elsewhere).
- Confidence: primary (digit-identical across two independently OCR'd and
  independently zoom-verified copies of the same wire dispatch).
- `comparable: true` (unlike the 1934 addendum's ceiling, this is a genuine
  night count and should be usable in the chart/CSV once promoted).
