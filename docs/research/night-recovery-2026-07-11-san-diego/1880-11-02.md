# 1880-11-02 presidential general — San Diego County night count (San Diego Union, 1880-11-03)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1880-11-02, presidential general (Garfield-Arthur R,
  Hancock-English D, Weaver-Chambers Greenback on the California ballot).
  Same issue also carried a California Assembly-district-ish local race
  (Pacheco/Leach/Godfrey) and a Congressional race (Hendrick/Willey) — those
  are NOT summed here (task scope is President only). San Diego was in its
  pre-boom era still (the 1880s "boom" began ~1885-88); the returns cover the
  5 city wards plus 9 outlying county precincts.
- Calendar check: `https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=cl&cl=CL2.1880.11&sp=SDDU&e=...`
  lists `SDDU18801102` through `SDDU18801130` with gaps only on non-publishing
  days; `SDDU18801103` (the expected day-after issue) exists — no gap to
  document.
- Day-after issue: San Diego Union, Wednesday morning 1880-11-03, Volume XX,
  Number 3108. Issue OID `SDDU18801103` (38 sections). Issue URL:
  https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=SDDU18801103
- Masthead gate: PASS — running head on page 4 reads "THE SAN DIEGO UNION /
  Wednesday Morning, November 3" (`mirror/cdnc/screenshots/sd_18801103_p4_crop_masthead2.png`);
  CDNC's own issue title independently gives "3 November 1880, Volume XX,
  Number 3108." Nov 2 1880 was a Tuesday, so Nov 3 was correctly a Wednesday.
- Notably, this returns table is dated "UNION OFFICE, Nov. 3d, 3 a.m." —
  i.e. compiled only a few hours after polls closed, the earliest-timestamped
  of the three 1870s-80 dossiers in this wave (1872 and 1876 give no clock
  time at all).

## Numerator: "RETURNS FROM THE CITY AND COUNTY" precinct compilation (PRESIDENT)

Every digit below was read directly from page-image crops (never from OCR
alone); all crops were legible with no ambiguous digits.

### San Diego city, five wards

| Ward | Garfield (R) | Hancock (D) | Weaver (GB) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First | 16 | 29 | — |
| Second | 17 | 14 | 1 |
| Third | 89 | 61 | 7 |
| Fourth | 122 | 87 | 1 |
| Fifth | 55 | 10 | — |
| **Sum of our reads** | **299** | **201** | **9** |
| **Printed city RECAPITULATION** | **299** | **151** | **10** |

Our Garfield sum matches the printed city Recapitulation EXACTLY (299=299),
a strong cross-check that every Garfield digit was read correctly. Our
Hancock sum (201) does NOT match the printed Recapitulation (151) — a
50-vote gap. Every individual ward's Hancock digit was independently
re-zoomed and re-confirmed legible (see screenshots above); the printed
"151" itself was also re-zoomed and reads unambiguously as "151," not "201."
This looks like a period compositor/arithmetic error in the printed
Recapitulation line itself (our per-ward sum is more trustworthy, since it
survives independent digit-by-digit confirmation across 5 separate crops,
while the Recapitulation is a single hand-tallied line). Weaver: our sum (9)
vs. printed (10) is a 1-vote gap of the same minor-noise magnitude seen
throughout this wave (1872, 1876 dossiers).

### Our full resummation vs. the paper's printed "total of the foregoing precincts"

> "The total vote of the foregoing precincts stands as follows: Garfield,
> 345; Hancock, 260; Weaver, 10. Pacheco, 380; Leach, 267; Godfrey, 44.
> Hendrick, 401; Willey, 301. Majorities—Garfield, 185; Pacheco, 113;
> Hendrick, 100."

This printed grand total (345/260/10) does **not** reconcile cleanly with
either (a) the 9 outlying precincts alone (126/112, no Weaver) or (b) city +
outlying combined (city 299/201/9 [our sum] or 299/151/10 [printed city
recap], + outlying 126/112 = 425/313/9 or 425/263/10). The Weaver figure (10)
matches the CITY-only Weaver count exactly, which is the strongest textual
signal that this final total is meant to include the city — but the Garfield
and Hancock totals are well short of what city+outlying actually sums to
(Garfield short by 80; Hancock closer but still off). The printed
"Majorities—Garfield, 185" line is itself internally inconsistent with the
same paragraph's own Garfield/Hancock figures (345−260=85, not 185 — a
precise 100-vote discrepancy, i.e. this specific total block contains at
least one clear internal arithmetic error, most likely a compositor slip
under the 3 a.m. deadline pressure signaled by the dateline itself).

**Given this, the contest sum used as the reported floor is OUR OWN
resummation of every precinct row actually printed in the table** (per the
skill's "sum all candidates as printed" convention), not the paper's
internally-inconsistent grand-total sentence:

**Contest sum = city (299 Garfield + 201 Hancock + 9 Weaver = 509) + 9
outlying precincts (126 Garfield + 112 Hancock + 0 Weaver = 238) = 747
ballots** (Garfield 425, Hancock 313, Weaver 9), across San Diego city (5
wards) plus 9 named outlying precincts.

This is flagged prominently below and in the Human-verification section: the
paper's own printed grand total (615 = 345+260+10) is LOWER than our
resummation (747) and should not be used as the reported floor, since it
fails its own internal majority-arithmetic check while our resummation
passes the strongest available cross-check (exact Garfield match at the city
level).

## Precincts-reported basis

No "X of Y county precincts" figure is printed. The 3 a.m. dateline ("Union
Office, Nov. 3d, 3 a.m. — Returns have been received from the following
precincts of the city and county") explicitly frames this as a partial,
early-morning compilation — precincts not yet telegraphed in by 3 a.m. are
implicitly excluded, making 747 (or even the paper's own lower 615) a floor
by the article's own framing, not just by convention. Precincts-reported
ratio: **PENDING** — no county precinct-count total printed.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 747 ballots** in the presidential contest (Garfield
425 + Hancock 313 + Weaver 9), across San Diego city (5 wards) plus 9 named
outlying precincts (National, Campo, San Luis Rey, Forster, Lyons, Mt.
Fairview, Fall Brook, Pala, Temecula), using our own resummation of every
precinct row printed in the San Diego Union's Wednesday 1880-11-03 morning
edition (dateline "3 a.m."). This is the most internally-inconsistent of the
three 1870s-80 tables recovered in this wave: the paper's own printed
Recapitulation (city) and grand-total lines both contain arithmetic that
does not check out (see Gates), most plausibly a compositor error made
compiling multiple telegraph dispatches on a 3 a.m. deadline. Every
individual precinct-level digit, however, was independently legible and
re-confirmed at high zoom, so the 747 floor rests on the precinct table
itself rather than on the paper's own (unreliable) subtotals. Share of
certified final: **57.3%** (738 / 1,289, same-basis Garfield+Hancock only;
certified figure from `denominators.md` 1880-11-02 entry, Tribune Almanac
for 1881 p.41). This headline figure excludes Weaver on both sides of the
ratio because no certified San Diego Weaver count was found — Weaver's night
count (9, this paper) and the Almanac's printed statewide totals are
Garfield/Hancock-only with no Greenback column. If Weaver is added on an
uncertified/estimated basis (night floor 747 vs. an approximate all-candidate
denominator of ~1,308, using a newspaper-sourced Weaver estimate of ~19 —
see `denominators.md`), the share is materially unchanged at ≈57.1%, but that
comparison is not certified-vs-certified and is not the headline number.
