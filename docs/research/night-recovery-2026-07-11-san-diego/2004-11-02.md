## Category scoping check

Unlike the 1988 and 2008 figures (each scoped to a single ballot category —
absentee-only and provisional-only respectively), this quote explicitly
combines the two major outstanding categories in one figure: "**provisional
ballots AND absentee ballots dropped off yesterday**." It is attributed
directly to the county Registrar of Voters, and is framed as the reason "the
outcome may not be known for days or weeks" — i.e., presented as the
complete remaining bottleneck for the whole county's results, not one
subcategory among several unaddressed ones. No other outstanding category
(e.g., regular pre-processed mail absentees, damaged/write-in ballots) is
mentioned as separately remaining.

## Range convention

The quote gives a range, "150,000 to 200,000." Per the decision rules ("If
the quote gives a range, subtract the LARGE end for the floor"), the floor
uses **200,000**.

## Arithmetic

Certified total ballots cast (SoS SOV 2004, `denominators.md`): **1,145,035**.

1,145,035 − 200,000 = **945,035** → 945,035 / 1,145,035 = **82.5%** of
certified.

## Soundness argument

No extra argument is required by the decision rules for a sub-90% share, but
for context: 2004 is post-HAVA (California's provisional-ballot regime was
in full effect) and pre-Voter's Choice Act, an era where in-person/precinct
ballots were the large majority of the vote and typically counted overnight,
while provisional ballots (new, requiring eligibility verification) and
late-drop-off absentees were the recognized lagging categories — exactly
what this quote describes. The 82.5% share is plausible on its face and
requires no additional defense.

## Verdict

**FLOOR = 945,035 ballots (82.5% of certified 1,145,035).** Basis: certified
total minus the Registrar's own day-after combined
provisional-plus-absentee-dropoff remaining figure (large end of the stated
150,000-200,000 range). Confidence: `secondary` (news-derived), one of the
stronger floors in this batch given the explicit two-category combination
and direct registrar attribution.
