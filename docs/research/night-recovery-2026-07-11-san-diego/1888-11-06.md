# 1888-11-06 presidential general — San Diego County night count (San Diego Union, 1888-11-07)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1888-11-06, presidential general (Harrison-R, Cleveland-D,
  Fisk-Prohibition, Curtis-American on the California ballot). By 1888 San
  Diego was in the middle of its real-estate boom; the county had grown
  sharply since 1884 (certified county total rose from 1,965 in 1884 to 8,194
  in 1888, per `denominators.md`) and the paper's returns are correspondingly
  much fuller — a large printed table plus several outlying-precinct
  dispatches.
- Day-after issue: San Diego Union and Daily Bee, Wednesday morning
  1888-11-07, Volume XXXIV, Number 6437. Issue OID `SDDU18881107` (136
  sections). Issue URL: https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=SDDU18881107
- Masthead gate: PASS — running head on page 5 reads "THE SAN DIEGO UNION,
  WEDNESDAY MORNING, NOVEMBER 7, 1888" (visible in
  `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/sd_18881107_p5_ztable_pan2.png`); CDNC issue
  metadata independently gives "7 November 1888"; Nov 6 1888 (election day)
  was a Tuesday, so Nov 7 was correctly a Wednesday.

Internal-consistency checks (both pass):
- Sixth Ward column (174+130+10=314) vs. prose: "a total of 419 votes cast,
  and at 2 o'clock this morning only a few over 300 votes had been
  counted" — 314 sits right in the "a few over 300" range. ✓
- City-only Harrison-Cleveland gap from the table (1,771-1,153=618) vs. the
  prose's own trajectory: "The count up to 3 o'clock this morning shows a
  Republican majority in the city of 545, which complete returns will
  probably increase to at least 700" — 618 sits between the 3-o'clock
  snapshot (545) and the projected final (~700), exactly where a later,
  more-complete table snapshot should land. ✓

One inconsistency, noted but not "corrected" (recorded exactly as printed,
per the skill's discipline): the table's own printed **Majority column
reads 557** for President, versus 618 implied by the column sums above (a
gap of 61). The same pattern recurs one row down, Chief Justice (Beatty
1,712 vs. Searls 1,148 by column-sum = majority 564, versus the table's
printed 551, gap 13) — evidence this is a period compositor/arithmetic
inconsistency in the SOURCE (the Majority/Plurality columns were plausibly
filled in from an earlier snapshot than the finished precinct columns),
not a transcription error here; both rows show the same directional gap.

Caveat on San Pasqual: unlike Escondido/Lakeside/Monument, which name
"Harrison"/"Cleveland" explicitly, San Pasqual's one-line dispatch says only
"35 Republican votes to 13 Democratic votes." A parallel case, Coronado
precinct (in article 2.65, not this table), similarly reports "156 were
Republican, 87 Democratic, and 5 Prohibitionist" out of 248 total votes
polled — and that figure is NOT the President tally (Coronado's own
ELECTION RETURNS table column, already counted in Part 1 above, shows
Harrison 110/Cleveland 87/Fisk 5 for President specifically, a different,
smaller number than the 156/87/5 "party of the voter" prose figure covering
all down-ballot offices). Coronado's table column was used in Part 1 and its
prose 156/87/5 figure was correctly NOT added a second time. San Pasqual has
no separate table column to fall back on, so its party-line dispatch is
used here as the President-contest proxy (it appears in the same "Back
Country Solid for Harrison" dispatch series, immediately after Escondido's
named Harrison/Cleveland totals, in the same brief-report format small
precincts use) — flagged as lower-confidence than the four precincts with
named candidates.

**Outside-precincts sum = (136+47+4) + (35+13) + (9+13) + (45+15+5)
= 187 + 48 + 22 + 65 = 322.**

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 3,288 ballots** in the presidential contest (Harrison
1,996 [1,771 table + 136 Escondido + 35 San Pasqual + 9 Lakeside + 45
Monument] + Cleveland 1,241 [1,153 table + 47 + 13 + 13 + 15] + Fisk 51 [42
table + 4 Escondido + 5 Monument], across 17 named precincts), printed in
the San Diego Union's Wednesday 1888-11-07
morning edition. This is a substantially larger and more granular return
than 1884 (812 ballots, 9 precincts), consistent with the boom-era
population growth the assignment anticipated. National City's precinct is
explicitly unreported ("no figures can be given") and several of the city's
"heaviest precincts" were still being counted at press time ("only a few
over 300 votes had been counted" of 419 in the Sixth Ward alone), so 3,288
is a genuine floor, not a complete count. Share of certified final (8,194):
**3,288 / 8,194 ≈ 40.1%**.
