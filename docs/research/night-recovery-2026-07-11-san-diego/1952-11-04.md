**Oceanside Blade-Tribune (`BT19521105`)**, page 1 (OCR:
`mirror/cdnc/BT19521105/section_BT19521105.1.1.txt`):
- Page-1 ear: "PHONE 2181 — if paper is not delivered to you in Oceanside **by 5:45 p.m.**,

roughly 17-19 hours after polls closed. Under the campaign rule (`README.md`, "the

issue can only ever produce a CEILING.

## What each paper actually printed (and what is missing)

28th District Congressional Contest; Bulen Wins Assembly Seat". Content:
- **National** UP tally: "Returns from 129,313 of the 145,940 polling places in the nation
  gave: Eisenhower 30,569,209, Stevenson 24,619,223."
- **Escondido city only**: the table "HERE'S HOW ESCONDIDO VOTED IN RACES FOR PRESIDENT,
  ASSEMBLY, CONGRESS" (12 city precincts) — "Final unofficial figures compiled by The
  Times-Advocate for Escondido's 12 city precincts show Eisenhower had 2,223 votes to
  Governor Adlai Stevenson's 1,011."

  of 286** counted **in the San Diego area**, Utt..." plus a separate Orange-county breakout
  ("Orange county, with 293 out of 338 precincts counted") — the 28th CD straddled San Diego
  and Orange counties in 1952, so this is neither a county figure nor a single-seat
  county-wide contest. (In 1950 the district *was* coterminous with the county at 1,052
  precincts; by 1952 redistricting had broken that identity — one more reason the 1950 route
  does not reproduce.)

- **No San Diego COUNTY presidential total anywhere.** The article headed "Escondido Area
  Joins County and State In Rolling Up Republican Victory" names the county in its headline

**`BT19521105`** — headline "REPUBLICANS WIN IN GREAT LANDSLIDE". Content: national UP

**`NCT19521106` (day+2)** — swept with `search:county`; hits across 12 page OIDs, none
containing a county-level Eisenhower/Stevenson figure. The county compilation never appears.

## Why 1952 fails where 1950 succeeded (at least as a ceiling)

In 1950 both county papers ran the same United Press **county** compilation ("In 872
precincts of the county's 1,052, Warren had 100,482 to 51,586 for Roosevelt" / "SAN DIEGO
COUNTY VOTING RECORD"). In 1952 UP evidently moved no equivalent San Diego County
presidential roll-up onto the wire that these papers took, and neither paper's own desk
compiled one — each fell back to its own city's precincts plus the national picture. This
matches the parallel finding that the statewide-county-table newspaper format had generally
vanished by 1952 (the prior Sacramento Union / Long Beach Press-Telegram probe), and shows
the collapse was not merely a big-paper phenomenon: San Diego County's own local press
stopped printing county roll-ups too.
