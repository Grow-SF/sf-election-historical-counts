# 1902-11-04 gubernatorial general — San Diego County night count (San Diego Union, 1902-11-05)

Agent dossier, written as-you-go. Repo untouched (no commits).

- Election: Tuesday 1902-11-04, gubernatorial general (George C. Pardee-R, Franklin K.
  Lane-D, Gideon S. Brower-Soc, Theo. D. Kanouse-Proh on the statewide ballot; the
  Union's San Diego county recap prints exactly these four tickets).
- Day-after issue: San Diego Union and Daily Bee, Wednesday morning 1902-11-05, issue
  OID `SDDU19021105` (89 sections). Issue URL: https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=SDDU19021105
- Masthead gate: PASS — dateline "THE SAN DIEGO UNION WEDNESDAY MORNING, NOVEMBER 5
  1902" printed directly above the returns table on page 3 (visible in
  `mirror/cdnc/screenshots/sd_19021105_p3.png`); Nov 5 1902 was in fact a Wednesday.

Method note: `cdnc_zoom.js` (double-click-to-zoom via OpenSeadragon) did not work on
this issue — repeated calls with different FX/FY targets returned near-identical crops
(the double-click appears to be consumed by a text-selection layer instead of the OSD
zoom gesture, visible as green text-highlight artifacts in the outputs). Worked around
by calling `cdnc_shot.js` with a large emulated viewport (4800x7200 instead of the
1600x2400 default) plus a tight `clip` region — this pulls higher-resolution deep-zoom
tiles into the render and produces legible digits directly, no OSD interaction needed.

## Numerator: the printed county recap ("FOR GOVERNOR", target contest)

Verbatim, article `SDDU19021105.2.18`, recap box (vision-read from
`sd_19021105_p3_hires_gov4.png`):

> "The figures as far as they have been received are as follows, reference being made
> to the tabulated statement for more specific information:
> FOR GOVERNOR.
> Pardee ..................... 2,06[?]
> Lane ........................ 1,729
> Brower ........................ 358
> Kanouse ......................... 54"

| Candidate | Party | Votes | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| George C. Pardee | Republican | **2,06[?]** | last digit obscured by a printed pointing-hand ornament glyph sitting directly over it; "2,061" is the best reading, flagged |
| Franklin K. Lane | Democrat | **1,729** | clean read |
| Gideon S. Brower | Socialist | **358** | clean read (first digit confirmed as "3" not "5" at high res) |
| Theo. D. Kanouse | Prohibition | **54** | clean read |

No "Scattering" line is printed in this recap (the certified SoV carries a Scattering
figure of 6; not captured by this partial night count, immaterial to the sum).

**Contest sum = 2,061 + 1,729 + 358 + 54 = 4,202 ballots** counted in the governor
contest, per the Union's own county-desk compilation "as far as... received" by press
time of the Nov. 5 morning edition. This is explicitly a partial count: the same
article notes elsewhere "not many of the precincts have been heard from" (re:
Amendment 28) and "the county will have a majority against a Republican candidate who
will have been elected to [Congress]... as far as they have been received."

Same-recap contests, for context (not the target contest, not summed as ballot floors
here): Congress — Daniels 1,731, Smythe (digit uncertain, ~1,858 or ~1,368) [?]; For
Superior Judge — Torrance 2,046, Guy 1,854, Andrews 1,860, Conklin 1,873 (a 3-seat race,
correctly excluded as a ballot-count floor per the multi-seat rule).

## Precinct grid cross-check (section 2.17)

The full "Partial and Unofficial Returns" precinct grid spans roughly the top 40% of
page 3 and lists dozens of precinct rows under a ~20-column header (Governor is the
first data column). OCR on this table is essentially unusable (dense small type
defeated the engine — see the raw OCR dump, mostly noise). Given the recap box in
2.18 explicitly states it summarizes this same tabulated statement ("reference being
made to the tabulated statement for more specific information"), and reproducing an
independent row-by-row re-sum of ~20 columns x dozens of rows at this scan resolution
was not tractable in this pass, the recap figures are used directly as the numerator
rather than re-derived. This is a deviation from the row-by-row transcription used in
the 1920 dossier (a much larger, cleaner-printed table); flagged here for the record.

## Precincts-reported basis

- Not enumerated by the paper as an "X of Y precincts" figure (unlike the 1920 dossier).
  The prose confirms the count is partial: "not many of the precincts have been heard
  from," and returns were still arriving ("the latest returns coming in as late as
  4:30 this morning"). Treated as a below-100% partial county count consistent with a
  morning-after press deadline.
- Contest sum (4,202) is well below the certified county total for the same contest
  (6,459; see Gates below), consistent with a partial-precinct night count.

## Verdict

**Night count FLOOR = 4,202 ballots** in the governor contest (Pardee 2,061[?] + Lane
1,729 + Brower 358 + Kanouse 54), partial county returns ("as far as they have been
received") as compiled by the San Diego Union's county desk for its Nov. 5, 1902
morning edition. Share of certified final (6,459): **4,202 / 6,459 = 65.1%**.
