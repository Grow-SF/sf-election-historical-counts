## Category scoping check (the decisive issue)

The only countable figure — 50,000 to 100,000 — is explicitly and solely
scoped to **provisional ballots** ("voters used such ballots" refers back to
"provisional ballots" in the prior sentence). This is exactly the case the
decision rules name directly: "'N provisionals remain' ... alone does NOT
bound mail/other categories: compute NO floor from it unless another quote
from the same coverage establishes the other categories were done."

2008 is also the first year in this batch where Vote-By-Mail is the
DOMINANT non-precinct category, not a minor one: the certified SOV shows
573,169 VBM voters out of 1,245,947 total (46.0% VBM, per
`denominators.md`). No quote anywhere in the reviewed 2008 coverage
addresses whether VBM/absentee ballots were fully counted, partially
counted, or how many remained as of election night. Given VBM's size that
year, a floor computed from the provisional figure alone would silently
assume the entire VBM category was complete, which is neither stated nor
plausible on its face (by 2008, late-arriving and drop-off VBM ballots were
already a recognized multi-day-processing category statewide).

## Arithmetic (not used — shown only to demonstrate why it would be unsound)

Certified total ballots cast (SoS SOV 2008, `denominators.md`): **1,245,947**.

If the provisional figure were (incorrectly) treated as the only remaining
category: 1,245,947 − 100,000 (large end of range) = 1,145,947 → 92.0% of
certified. This number is NOT adopted as a floor; it is shown only to
illustrate that even the arithmetic would land in a band that itself
requires an explicit soundness argument this dossier cannot supply (no
evidence about VBM-ballot completion exists in the reviewed coverage).
