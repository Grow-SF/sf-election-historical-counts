## FINDING (2026-07-09) -- CEILING, not the plateau

Bernardino, CA), docref news/18DB36E88190ECB8): "In San Bernardino County, as of Wednesday at 4
p.m., the Registrar of Voters had counted ballots cast by 218,993 people. An estimated 232,000
mail-in ballots still need to be processed, along with 1,3[00-ish] ... about 1,400 more
unprocessed ballots with other issues, including damage." Also: "The San Bernardino County
Registrar of Voters is scheduled to release its next set of results at 4 p.m. on today [Thursday]."

This 218,993/4-p.m.-Wednesday figure matches EXACTLY the county's own posting schedule slot this
row's existing note already identified as the FIRST CANVASS UPDATE (canvass resuming at "the 4
p.m. Wed posting", per the previously-recovered archived Results Posting Schedule) -- i.e. this is
confirmed to be AFTER the county's actual 9:40 a.m. Wednesday "Final Unofficial Election Night
Results" posting, not the plateau itself. The true 9:40 a.m. figure remains unarchived (Wayback
only has the 4 a.m. intermediate, 177,481/49.37% precincts) and unquoted in any found article.

Since counting only increases monotonically, this gives a genuine BRACKET: 177,481 (4 a.m., known
mid-count undercount, too far below to use even as a floor) <= true 9:40 a.m. plateau <= 218,993
(4 p.m., first canvass update). No article states the 9:40 a.m. figure directly. Searched
"9:40 a.m." / "218,993" / "Final Unofficial" across SB Sun 11/09-11/11: only image-only full-page
scans (no extractable article text) and the same already-examined article.

DECISION: land 218,993 as a documented CEILING (upper bound on the unrecoverable true plateau),
comparable:false, confidence secondary, plateau_review REFUTED_AS_PLATEAU (confirmed, by this
county's own posting schedule, to be the canvass-resumption figure, not election night).
