## FINDING (2026-07-09) -- CEILING, not a clean plateau

SB Sun's 2014 election-night coverage has no countywide ballot-count or turnout-percent
statement (7 hits examined: mostly local council/school-board races; one district-specific
figure, "More than 15,000 ballots are still to be counted in House race" for CD-31, docref
news/1516DEC90B0380F8, quoting "As of 1:32 a.m., with all precincts reporting" -- genuine
election-night timing, but district-specific, not countywide, and not usable as a county total).

Cross-check via Press-Enterprise (already mirrored while researching riverside-2014.md):
"a new low in area's Voter turnout?" (Nov 6, 2014, docref news/151717B8F7250120): "Less than 30
percent of registered voters cast ballots in the Inland area, according to PRELIMINARY NUMBERS
RELEASED WEDNESDAY BY COUNTY REGISTRARS ... In San Bernardino County, turnout was 27.1 percent.
San Bernardino County Registrar of Voters Michael Scarpello estimated that the final number
likely will be closer to 34 percent" (matches actual certified 34.44% almost exactly, validating
the article).

27.1% x 851,684 registered = 230,806. 230,806 / 293,283 certified = 78.70% -- anomalously high
(similar to the SB 2012 row's 82.19% ceiling), and this county's own already-documented posting
schedule (from the existing dead-end note, sourced from resultsTimeline.html) shows SB updates
precinct counts "every 2hrs" through Wednesday -- meaning a "Wednesday" turnout figure plausibly
already includes several post-election-night updates, NOT a frozen overnight count. Same
treatment as SB 2012: land as a documented CEILING, comparable:false, confidence secondary,
plateau_review REFUTED_AS_PLATEAU (post-night framing + known continuous-update schedule).
