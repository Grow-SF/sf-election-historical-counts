## Outcome

Landed: election_night_ballots = 22,012, certified_final = 27,370, pct =
80.42%, confidence secondary, comparable (not flagged false).

Both Fresno Bee and Madera Tribune agree the sheriff and DA races reached
100 of 100 precincts reporting Tuesday night (election night), but neither
paper prints a raw countywide ballot total for the night itself, only
percentages. The Madera Tribune's Friday Nov 7 2014 follow-up, "More than
5,300 votes left to count" (pA1, docref news/15175914B6DDC6C0), quotes
Clerk-Recorder Rebecca Martinez quantifying exactly what remained
uncounted: 4,385 late vote-by-mail ballots (received after noon on Election
Day, too late for the night's tally) + 973 provisional ballots (requiring
eligibility verification) = "the 5,358 ballots left to be counted." Since
certified final is 27,370, this yields 27,370 - 5,358 = 22,012 ballots
counted as of election night, a FLOOR per RUNBOOK 5.2, corroborated by the
same article's own "more than 22,000 votes" framing and by the row's
existing Friday-2:14pm-capture datapoint (26,449, which is 4,437 higher than
22,012, closely matching the 4,385-ballot late-VBM batch the article says
would be processed "by 5 tonight").

Calibration: 80.42% sits alongside 2016's 80.0% (both pre-e-pollbook,
traditional-polling-place era), a stronger fit than the brief's a-priori
65-80% midterm estimate, explained by the small precinct-based county
counting fast in the pre-VCA era.

Plateau verdict: PLAUSIBLE (self-description consistent: 100/100 precincts,
a clerk-quantified not-yet-counted breakdown; no independent version bracket
available since Madera's Clarity ENR did not exist until 2018).
