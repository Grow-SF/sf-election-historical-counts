## FINDING (2026-07-09)

"Council members succeed, struggle" (Nov 6, 2014 Press-Enterprise, docref news/151717B89F05D948,
byline Sandra Stokley/Peter Fischetti): "The Riverside County Registrar of Voters office
announced that 93,000 votes are still to be processed, including 66,000 vote-by-mail ballots,
19,000 provisional ballots and 8,000 damaged ballots. The next update will be posted at 6 p.m.
tonight." (Same Riverside pattern as 2012: held count since election night, first post-night
update posted at 6 p.m. two days later.) Multiple local races in the same article cite "all N
precincts reporting" confirming the countywide precinct-report milestone had been reached.

Independent cross-check, "a new low in area's Voter turnout?" (same date, docref
news/151717B8F7250120, byline David Olson): "In Riverside County, turnout was 29.8 percent as of
Wednesday. It likely will end up at about 39 or 40 percent once all the ballots are counted, said
Registrar Rebecca Spencer." CA SoS 2014 SOV confirms final Riverside registration = 891,575 and
final turnout = 40.13% (matching Spencer's "39 or 40 percent" estimate almost exactly, validating
the article's other figures). 29.8% x 891,575 = 265,689, within 1,000 ballots (0.3%) of the
93,000-derived 264,764 -- an independent, non-circular cross-check from a second bylined article
using a completely different method (turnout percentage x registration, vs certified-minus-
outstanding).

PLATEAU = certified final 357,764 minus 93,000 outstanding = 264,764.
264,764 / 357,764 = 74.01%. High end of the ~55-75% band but corroborated two independent ways.

LANDED: election_night_ballots 264764, pct 74.01, confidence secondary, comparable true,
plateau_review CONFIRMED (posting-schedule bracket + independent turnout-percentage cross-check).
