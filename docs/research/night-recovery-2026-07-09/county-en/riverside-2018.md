## FINDING (2026-07-09)

"Elections - Many Inland ballots still not processed or counted" (Nov 8, 2018 Press-Enterprise,
docref news/16F9051D0AED5530, byline Johnny Bender) covers BOTH Riverside and San Bernardino in
one regional piece, with DIRECT overnight-Tuesday counted figures for each county (not a
subtraction derivation):

- "Final unofficial vote counts aren't expected to be released until 6 p.m. today for Riverside
  County and 4 p.m. Friday for San Bernardino County."
- Riverside: "Riverside County Registrar of Voters Rebecca Spencer said she had a crew of about
  300 workers processing ballots overnight Tuesday. According to Wednesday vote totals, they
  counted about 300,000 ballots. On Wednesday morning, Spencer's staff started going over about
  200,000 vote-by-mail, 45,000 provisional and 5,000 damaged ballots." Spencer, quoted the same
  Wednesday: "We're not counting anything today ... We are sorting out all the materials from the
  polling places" -- i.e. the registrar herself confirms the 300,000 figure is FROZEN through the
  article's Wednesday writing (no counting happens that day), a strong self-confirming plateau.
- San Bernardino (used for the sb-2018 row, see san-bernardino-2018.md): "elections workers
  counted about 322,000 ballots overnight Tuesday. On Wednesday, about 150,816 ballots remained
  to be counted, along with 8,929 damaged ballots and 43,818 provisional and other ballots" (a
  Wednesday remaining total of 203,563, lower than the Tuesday-night-implied remaining of 224,041
  = 546,041-322,000, consistent with a small amount of Wednesday-daytime processing before this
  article's data cutoff -- direction/magnitude both make sense, not a contradiction).

PLATEAU (Riverside) = 300,000 directly stated ("counted about 300,000 ballots" overnight Tuesday,
per Wednesday vote totals, frozen per Spencer's own "not counting anything today" quote).

"mid-40s-to-60s on-night shares are plausible" carve-out for Riverside's notoriously slow 2010s
counting (2018 also saw unusually high turnout/lines per this same article, plausibly slowing
the count further).
