## CONCLUSION (2012-11-06)

Source-scoped U-T search (both the coverage file's own probe and my repeat of it,
plus a widened Nov 1-30 2012 window with no text term) confirms San Diego
Union-Tribune, The (CA) is genuinely empty in NewsBank for this entire window;
this is a real archive hole, not a query artifact (consistent with the Task 1
coverage file's diagnostic). Per the task brief, fell back to an
all-publications (no source restriction) search for `"San Diego County"` combined
with ballots/registrar/precincts terms in the 11/06-11/10/2012 window.

**Plateau found via all-publications search:**
- US Fed News (USA), Nov 9 2012, docref news/14273902C00B3B00, headline
  "REGISTRAR'S OFFICE TALLYING FINAL VOTES", explicitly republishes VERBATIM
  a San Diego County press release dated Nov. 7: "Ballots from the precincts
  are taken to the ROV and results are released as they are processed. Those
  ballots made up about 63 percent of the total vote. The remaining 37 percent
  of ballots - about 475,000 of them - are vote-by-mail and provisional
  ballots" (breakdown: ~100,000 mail arrived Mon/Tue + ~275,000 mail dropped
  off Tue + ~90,000 provisional Tue). "New numbers expected to be released at
  5 p.m. Thursday" - i.e. this press release describes the state BEFORE any
  post-night update, still election night's number.
- North County Times (Escondido, CA), Nov 6 2012, docref news/1426A3C9A60D1AA8,
  "Peters expanding his lead over Bilbray": "Peters took the lead with all
  precincts reporting ... according to figures released by the county
  registrar about 2:25 a.m. Election officials said about 475,000 absentee
  and provisional ballots from across the county remain uncounted." This is
  the same 475,000 figure, sourced to the 2:25 a.m. post-midnight report
  (still election night per RUNBOOK section 1's 1-4am rule), with ALL
  precincts reporting.
- The Nov 7 press release (wired Nov 9) restates the SAME 475,000 figure as
  the 2:25 a.m. report, without any newer number yet (next update scheduled
  Thursday 5pm) - this is the "held" non-circular leg: the county's own
  later statement confirms the count had not moved since election night.

**Arithmetic (plateau derivation):** certified final 1,203,265 minus the
registrar-quantified 475,000 outstanding mail/provisional ballots = 728,265
counted as of the 2:25 a.m. election-night report. 728,265 / 1,203,265 =
60.52%. Cross-check: certified VBM total was 675,007; if ~475,000 of that
(plus provisional) was outstanding, ~200,000 VBM had already been processed
by election night, plus the fully-counted 528,258 precinct ballots =
~728,258, matching the derived 728,265 within rounding. NOT the 8pm first
tranche (which would be far lower, before any precinct or absentee
processing at all). Comparable: true (this is a genuine election-night
value, not a days-later canvass derivation - the "held" 2:25am-through-Nov7
evidence rules that out).
