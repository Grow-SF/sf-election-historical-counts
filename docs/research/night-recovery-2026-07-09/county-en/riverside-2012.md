## FINDING (2026-07-09)

Two Nov 8, 2012 Press-Enterprise articles (both covering the Nov 6 election) independently
state the same countywide "183,000" ballots-remaining figure:

- "Buster facing defeat" (docref news/14270915258D4BE8, byline Jeff Horseman): "Unofficial final
  results from the county Registrar of Voters show Kevin Jeffries leading Buster ... with all
  precincts reporting. But about 183,000 vote-by-mail, provisional and damaged ballots remained
  to be counted." Also: "Buster ... didn't concede Wednesday, Nov. 7" (i.e. the count had not
  moved as of Wednesday).
- "Bono Mack, Baca ousters hit clout" (docref news/14270914E8F945E8, byline Ben Goad): "With all
  precincts reporting, the Palm Springs Republican trailed Ruiz ... Bono Mack said she prefers to
  await the final tally of 183,000 mail-in, provisional and damaged ballots that remained to be
  counted countywide. ... The Riverside County voter registrar's office is expected to issue an
  updated vote count at 6 p.m. Thursday, Nov. 8."

Both articles describe the SAME state (all precincts reporting, 183,000 countywide remaining) and
both confirm no update had occurred between election night and their Wednesday/Thursday-morning
print deadline, with the registrar's NEXT update explicitly scheduled for Thursday 6 p.m. This is
the San Diego 2012 pattern (certified minus a stated, held, pre-update outstanding count = the
election-night plateau), not a post-night canvass derivation: the schedule itself brackets the
report (RUNBOOK 8 "county's own posting schedule bracketing the report").

PLATEAU = certified final 669,627 minus 183,000 outstanding = 486,627 counted.
486,627 / 669,627 = 72.67%. Within the brief's ~55-75% precinct-era calibration band (high end,
consistent with Riverside's own "slow counter" reputation being about VBM-heavy LATER canvass
days, not about the initial precincts-plus-early-VBM tranche).

LANDED: election_night_ballots 486627, pct 72.67, confidence secondary, comparable true,
